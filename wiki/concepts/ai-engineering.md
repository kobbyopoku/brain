# AI Engineering

> The discipline of building software systems around AI models — treated as a specialization of software engineering rather than a separate or beginner field.

## Definition

AI engineering is framed as the practice of building reliable systems *around* models rather than chasing individual tools or merely using AI products. [[wiki/sources/techwithtimm-ai-engineer-roadmap-2026]] characterizes it as "still software engineering" — building systems around models — and explicitly not a beginner field. [[wiki/sources/Shruti_0810-zero-to-ai-engineer-roadmap]] frames the goal as understanding how modern AI works and deploying systems that solve real problems, distinct from simply using AI tools.

## Why it matters

AI engineering sits at the center of Godwin's work cluster (AI products + AI services, agentic architecture). Both roadmap sources treat it as a learnable, sequenced career path, making the concept a hub for the prerequisite sub-skills (prompt engineering, fine-tuning, embeddings, vector databases, context windows, LLMOps).

## Treatment across sources

- [[wiki/sources/techwithtimm-ai-engineer-roadmap-2026]] frames it as the discipline the whole source defines and sequences; "still software engineering" — building systems around models, not chasing tools. Not a beginner field.
- [[wiki/sources/Shruti_0810-zero-to-ai-engineer-roadmap]] frames it as the discipline the entire source sequences as a ~14-week learning path; the goal is to understand how modern AI works and deploy systems that solve real problems, not merely use AI tools.

## Sub-claims and details

- AI engineering is positioned as a specialization of software engineering, not a field apart from it ([[wiki/sources/techwithtimm-ai-engineer-roadmap-2026]]).
- It is explicitly not a beginner field ([[wiki/sources/techwithtimm-ai-engineer-roadmap-2026]]).
- Shruti's roadmap structures the discipline as a ~14-week learning path ([[wiki/sources/Shruti_0810-zero-to-ai-engineer-roadmap]]).
- The objective is deploying systems that solve real problems, distinguished from merely using AI tools ([[wiki/sources/Shruti_0810-zero-to-ai-engineer-roadmap]]).

## Open questions and contradictions

- The two roadmaps differ in granularity (staged sequence vs. ~14-week timeline); whether they converge on the same skill set in the same order needs a closer comparison.
