# Proof of Authority (Agent Legal Authority)

> A machine-readable, signed declaration of what an autonomous agent is allowed to bind its principal to.

## Definition

Proof of authority is a machine-readable, signed declaration specifying what an agent is permitted to commit its principal (the person or org it acts for) to. [[wiki/sources/awrigh01-technical-stack-autonomous-agents]] presents it as the bridge between technical permissions (agent governance, Layer 8) and enforceable contracts (Layer 5), and frames it as a genuinely unsolved problem.

## Why it matters

Without it, agent-signed commitments carry no reliable legal force. The source's epigram: "Every agent-signed contract is one *ultra vires* claim away from worthless" — i.e., a counterparty can always argue the agent acted beyond its granted authority and void the deal.
