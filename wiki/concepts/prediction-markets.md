# Prediction markets

> Markets where participants take positions on the outcome of future events, treated by the canonical source here not as bet pools but as order books with spreads, liquidity, and exploitable structure.

## Definition

A prediction market lets participants take positions on the outcome of a future event. The single ingested source treats them as financial order books — with bid-ask spreads and liquidity — rather than as simple bet pools, and frames them as having exploitable structure.

## Treatment across sources

- [[wiki/sources/retrochainer-claude-700k-musk-tweets]] frames it as: not bet pools but order books with spreads, liquidity, and exploitable structure. The source's thesis is to ask "how do I earn regardless of outcome?" rather than "what will the outcome be?"

## Sub-claims and details
