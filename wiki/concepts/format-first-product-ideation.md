# Format-First Product Ideation

> A product-ideation inversion: pick the viral content format first, then build the minimum app that format requires.

## Definition

**Format-First Product Ideation** inverts traditional ideation. Instead of conceiving a product and then finding distribution, the operator first selects a content format that is Viral + Scalable + Sustainable, then builds the minimum app the format requires. [[wiki/sources/mattgittleson-vibecoded-b2c-375k-exit]] describes two routes: Path A studies apps that are already making revenue; Path B steals proven ecommerce or info-product formats and attaches a product with better economics.

## Why it matters

For a vibecoded B2C app, distribution — not engineering — is the binding constraint. Anchoring the product to a proven viral format de-risks the hardest part (will anyone see it?) before any build effort, which is the source's account of how it reached a fast exit. It connects directly to the source's distribution engine.

## Sub-claims and details

- The format-selection test is three-part: Viral + Scalable + Sustainable. ([[wiki/sources/mattgittleson-vibecoded-b2c-375k-exit]])
- The app is scoped to the minimum the chosen format requires. ([[wiki/sources/mattgittleson-vibecoded-b2c-375k-exit]])
- Path A: study apps already making revenue. ([[wiki/sources/mattgittleson-vibecoded-b2c-375k-exit]])
- Path B: steal proven ecommerce/info-product formats, attach a product with better economics. ([[wiki/sources/mattgittleson-vibecoded-b2c-375k-exit]])

## Open questions and contradictions

- The source presents this as a candidate concept from a single case; it is not yet corroborated by other sources. Whether format-first generalizes beyond short-form-video-driven B2C is unsettled.
