# UGC Marketing

> Using paid user-generated-content creators — micro-influencers running app-dedicated accounts — as a distribution channel for mobile apps.

## Definition

UGC marketing recruits creators to produce native-feeling, user-generated-style content that promotes a product. In [[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]] it is the practice of hiring micro-influencers and new creators to run accounts dedicated to a single app, optimizing for creators who can make content go viral rather than for existing follower counts.

## Why it matters

The source frames UGC as the highest-leverage and hardest mobile distribution channel — high upside, but operationally demanding. The wiki tracks it as one of the four channels in [[wiki/concepts/mobile-app-distribution]] and as directly relevant to small-team growth.

## Treatment across sources

- [[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]] frames UGC creators as the highest-leverage and hardest mobile distribution channel: hire micro-influencers/new creators to run app-dedicated accounts; followers don't matter — look for 2–3 viral videos plus camera charisma; and running a "UGC army" is a full-time job (recruit, monitor, fire fast, pay).

## Open questions and contradictions

- The source gives selection signals (viral videos, charisma) but no pay rates or team-size guidance for the "UGC army."
