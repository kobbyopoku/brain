# The Organization Man

> William Whyte's archetype of the white-collar middle manager who "took the vows of organization life" — the central figure of the future-of-work essay, declared obsolete.

## Definition

The Organization Man is William Whyte's archetype of the white-collar middle manager who "took the vows of organization life." In [[wiki/sources/kushwah-aaryan-future-of-work]] this archetype is the essay's central figure: it is declared "now dead" and "a temporary shape." The page is filed as a concept (a named archetype) rather than an entity because the wiki's `entity_type` vocabulary lacks an archetype value.

## Why it matters

The archetype gives a human face to the salaried-firm form whose disappearance the essay argues for. Declaring the Organization Man "a temporary shape" is the rhetorical anchor for the broader claim that the hierarchical salaried corporation is an accident, not the natural state.
