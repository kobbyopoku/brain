# Design System

> A brand's blueprint — its specific colors, fonts, button styles, spacing, and overall style — which, once established and connected in an AI design tool, makes every subsequent output automatically on-brand and consistent.

## Definition

In the framing of [[wiki/sources/prajwaltomar-claude-design-workflow]], a **design system** is "your brand's blueprint": a defined set of specific colors, fonts, button styles, spacing, and overall style that captures how a brand should look. Once this system is connected in Claude Design, every output is automatically on-brand and consistent. It is treated as the always-first foundation step of the workflow, built via a structured prompt and refined through a "needs-work" iteration loop.

## Why it matters

The design system is the mechanism that replaces ad-hoc, per-output styling decisions with a single up-front establishment. By pre-establishing the blueprint, the workflow avoids the generic, shifting output and token-burning iteration of [[vibe-designing]] — the design system is the concrete artifact whose absence the anti-pattern describes.
