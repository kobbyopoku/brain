# Data moat

> A defensible competitive advantage built from accumulated proprietary data that a competitor cannot acquire off the shelf.

## Definition

A **data moat** is the durable advantage an AI-services operator accumulates as its [[data-flywheel]] runs — proprietary, operationally-earned data that a pure-play SaaS competitor cannot copy. In [[wiki/sources/itsalexvacca-3-phases-ai-layer]] it is the asset produced by "the loop," and it also lowers the marginal cost of delivering each additional client.

## Why it matters

The data moat is the economic payoff of running the [[data-flywheel]]: it both defends against competitors and flattens cost per delivered client over time, which is central to the services-as-software thesis.

## Treatment across sources

- [[wiki/sources/itsalexvacca-3-phases-ai-layer]] frames it as a moat that the loop builds, one a SaaS pure-play *"can't copy off the shelf,"* and notes it flattens cost per delivered client over campaigns.

## Sub-claims and details

- A SaaS pure-play cannot copy the moat off the shelf ([[wiki/sources/itsalexvacca-3-phases-ai-layer]]).
- The moat flattens cost per delivered client over successive campaigns ([[wiki/sources/itsalexvacca-3-phases-ai-layer]]).

## Open questions and contradictions

- Unresolved by current sources: how the moat is measured or quantified, and at what scale it becomes meaningfully defensible.
