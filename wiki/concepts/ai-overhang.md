# AI overhang / arbitrage window

> The gap between what people think AI can do and what it actually can do — an arbitrage window the market pays a practitioner to close, and one the source argues is closing as the market catches up.

## Definition

The **AI overhang** (or **arbitrage window**) is the core framing concept of [[wiki/sources/exploraX_-5-solo-ai-business-models]]. It is the gap between *perceived* AI capability (what the general market thinks AI can do) and *actual* AI capability (what it can already do today). That gap is an arbitrage opportunity: the market will pay someone who can operate inside the gap — building things people don't yet realize are buildable. The window is framed as **closing** as the broader market catches up to actual capability.

## Why it matters

This is the framing that motivates all five solo-AI business models in the source: each model is a way to monetize the overhang before it closes. The "closing window" framing is also the source's urgency driver — the argument for acting now rather than later. It pairs with [[distribution-as-moat]] as the source's two load-bearing concepts: the overhang explains *why there is money to be made*, while distribution explains *what actually determines who captures it*.

- **Monetization**: the market pays practitioners to close the gap ([[wiki/sources/exploraX_-5-solo-ai-business-models]]).
- **Timing**: the window is closing as the market catches up — used as an urgency driver ([[wiki/sources/exploraX_-5-solo-ai-business-models]]).

## Open questions and contradictions

- The "window is closing" claim is an assertion of timing without a stated horizon or evidence. It is a recurring rhetorical move in AI-business content; worth treating as motivational framing rather than a forecast until a source quantifies it.
