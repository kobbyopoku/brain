# Agentic Capital Markets

> A market in the hard sense — where autonomous economic actors can be authorized, discovered, priced, contracted with, paid, verified, and governed — defined as three planes (trust, market, control) across ten layers.

## Definition

Agentic Capital Markets is the central framing of [[wiki/sources/awrigh01-technical-stack-autonomous-agents]]: a market in the hard sense where autonomous economic actors can be authorized, discovered, priced, contracted with, paid, verified, and governed. The source defines it as three planes — trust, market, and control — across ten layers. Its guiding reframing: "not who funds an agent, but what allows one software actor to reliably transact with another."

## Why it matters

The framing treats the agent economy as a stack of infrastructure problems rather than a funding question, naming each layer as a buildable component. For Godwin's interests in agentic architecture and infrastructure, it provides a map of where the unbuilt pieces (and openings) are.

## Treatment across sources

## Sub-claims and details

- The market is defined across three planes: trust, market, and control ([[wiki/sources/awrigh01-technical-stack-autonomous-agents]]).
- The stack comprises ten layers ([[wiki/sources/awrigh01-technical-stack-autonomous-agents]]).
- The central question is "not who funds an agent, but what allows one software actor to reliably transact with another" ([[wiki/sources/awrigh01-technical-stack-autonomous-agents]]).
- Autonomous economic actors must be able to be authorized, discovered, priced, contracted with, paid, verified, and governed ([[wiki/sources/awrigh01-technical-stack-autonomous-agents]]).

## Open questions and contradictions

- The source positions several layers as unbuilt; the full mapping of which planes contain which of the ten layers is not enumerated in the cited note.
