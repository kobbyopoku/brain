# One-person company

> The thesis that AI leverage drives the minimum viable team for a high-revenue internet business toward a single person, collapsing the distinction between creator, SaaS founder, and e-commerce operator.

## Definition

A **one-person company**, in the framing of [[wiki/sources/kushwah-aaryan-future-of-work]], is the endpoint of a trend in which the minimum viable team for a $10M-ARR internet business drops toward one. The source's central thesis is that creator, SaaS founder, and e-commerce operator are "the same animal now" — distinct historical roles converging into a single solo operator empowered by AI leverage.

## Why it matters

The concept is a direct statement about how AI changes the economics of starting and running a business: if the team floor approaches one, the constraints on who can build a high-revenue internet business shift dramatically. For a solo or small-team builder, it reframes the relevant question from "what team do I need" to "what leverage do I have." It sits adjacent to the wiki's existing solo-business clusters.

## Treatment across sources

- [[wiki/sources/kushwah-aaryan-future-of-work]] frames it as the central thesis: the minimum viable team for a $10M-ARR internet business drops toward one; creator = SaaS founder = e-commerce operator are "the same animal now."

## Open questions and contradictions

- The source asserts the trend toward a one-person team but the cited material here does not detail the mechanisms (which functions are automated, which are outsourced) that make $10M ARR solo-achievable.
