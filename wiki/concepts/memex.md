# Memex

> Vannevar Bush's 1945 vision of a personal, actively curated knowledge store with associative trails between documents — a conceptual ancestor of the [[llm-wiki-pattern]].

## Definition

The memex was a thought experiment by [[wiki/entities/vannevar-bush]] in 1945: a personal device on which an individual would store all their books, articles, and notes, and — crucially — link them via *associative trails* that mirrored how the mind itself draws connections. The trails were as valuable as the documents themselves, and the store was private, actively maintained, and lifelong.

## Why it matters

The memex prefigures the [[llm-wiki-pattern]]: a private, actively curated knowledge store where the connections between documents are first-class. Karpathy explicitly cites it as the spiritual ancestor in [[wiki/sources/llm-wiki-pattern-karpathy]]. The piece Bush could not solve in 1945 — *who maintains the trails as the corpus grows?* — is the part LLMs now answer.

The public web that emerged later took some surface features of the memex (hyperlinks, large corpora) but inverted the rest: it became public rather than private, populated by everyone rather than curated by oneself, and the trails became search results rather than personal associations. The LLM Wiki pattern is closer to Bush's original vision than to the web.

## Treatment across sources

- [[wiki/sources/llm-wiki-pattern-karpathy]] — invoked in the closing section as a spiritual predecessor. Karpathy frames the LLM specifically as solving the maintenance problem the memex left open.

## Sub-claims and details

- **Private, not public**: the memex was for one person, not a shared knowledge commons.
- **Actively curated**: the user shapes the store deliberately.
- **Associative trails are first-class**: connections are explicit, persistent, navigable artifacts — not derived per-query.
