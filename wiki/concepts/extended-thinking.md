# Extended Thinking

> A Claude reasoning mode in which the model is prompted (or toggled) to deliberate visibly before answering, reserved for complex decisions.

## Definition

Extended Thinking is treated as a deliberate reasoning mode for Claude, engaged either via a brain-icon toggle in the interface or by prompting the model to "think step by step, show reasoning, flag uncertainty." It is positioned as a tool for complex decisions rather than routine queries.

## Why it matters

It sits in the cluster of practical Claude-usage techniques the wiki tracks. For Godwin's AI-product and agentic-architecture work, knowing when to invoke explicit reasoning versus fast responses is a usage-efficiency lever.
