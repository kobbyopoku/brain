# Reactions-to-AI opportunity pattern

> A recurring class of startup opportunities that are counter-positions against AI saturation — businesses serving the human needs that AI's spread amplifies rather than AI products themselves.

## Definition

The reactions-to-AI opportunity pattern names a cluster of startup ideas whose value comes not from building AI, but from reacting to a world saturated with it. Surfaced as a meta-observation over a list of startup opportunities, the pattern groups ventures around loneliness, belonging, human verification, analog/nostalgia, and in-real-life (IRL) community — needs that grow more acute as AI proliferates. The thesis is that some of the biggest openings are counter-positions to AI rather than AI products.

## Why it matters

It reframes "what to build in the AI era" away from the default (ship an AI product) toward the second-order question (what does ubiquitous AI make scarce or valuable?). For an operator scanning opportunity lists, it is a lens that surfaces durable demand — human connection, trust, authenticity — that is structurally insulated from AI commoditization.

## Sub-claims and details

- The pattern is articulated as a reader's meta-observation (Thomas Power) over a startup-opportunity list, not as the list author's stated thesis. [[wiki/sources/gregisenberg-36-startup-opportunities]]
- Named instances of the counter-position: loneliness, belonging, human verification, analog/nostalgia, and IRL community. [[wiki/sources/gregisenberg-36-startup-opportunities]]
- The core claim is that the biggest opportunities are reactions *against* AI saturation rather than AI products. [[wiki/sources/gregisenberg-36-startup-opportunities]]

## Open questions and contradictions

- The pattern is drawn from a single commenter on one list; whether it generalizes beyond that list is untested by the wiki's current sources.
