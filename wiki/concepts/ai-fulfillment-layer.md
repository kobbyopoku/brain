# AI fulfilment layer

> The stack of AI capabilities that actually delivers a services-as-software offering — composed of a data spine, agents, and a feedback loop — and the line item that decides whether the business scales or stays a body shop.

## Definition

The AI fulfilment layer is the part of a services-as-software business that does the work: the AI system that fulfils what the company sells. It is composed as spine → agents → loop — a data spine, the agents that act on it, and a feedback loop that improves them. Across a prior framing it spans five sub-layers — data, outreach, orchestration, reporting, and knowledge. The distinguishing contribution of the anchor source is not the inventory of layers but the *sequencing*: which to build first.

## Why it matters

In a services-as-software model, the fulfilment layer is the line item that sets the ceiling on the business. Build it well and the firm scales as software; get it wrong and it remains, in the source's phrase, "a body shop with a logo." The build-order question — what to build first — is therefore framed as the highest-leverage decision a services-as-software founder makes. This connects directly to [[services-as-software]] and to the human's AI-services work.
