# Claude-as-infrastructure

> Using Claude not as a chatbot but as an infrastructure tool that translates plain-language strategy descriptions into working code, removing the need for a development team or months of programming.

## Definition

Claude-as-infrastructure is the framing in which Claude is used "not as a chatbot, but as an infrastructure tool" — a component that turns plain-language descriptions of a strategy into working code. The canonical source presents this as the means by which a non-engineer can ship a working automation "no development team, no months of programming."

## Treatment across sources

- [[wiki/sources/retrochainer-claude-700k-musk-tweets]] frames it as: the source's central Claude claim — Claude used "not as a chatbot, but as an infrastructure tool" that translates plain-language strategy descriptions into working bot code, "no development team, no months of programming." This is the cleanest statement of the framing in the wiki so far and pairs with [[reasoning-execution-split]].
