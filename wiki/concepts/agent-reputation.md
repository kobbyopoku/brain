# Agent Reputation

> Layer 3 of the agentic capital markets stack: the mechanism by which non-commoditized agent markets clear on reputation, requiring attested track records, portability, and Sybil resistance.

## Definition

Agent Reputation is Layer 3 of the agentic capital markets stack in [[wiki/sources/awrigh01-technical-stack-autonomous-agents]]. Markets clear on reputation when goods are not commoditized. A functioning layer needs cryptographically attested outcome track records, cross-marketplace portability, and Sybil resistance. Portability is blocked because reputation is the marketplace's moat. ERC-8004's Reputation Registry stores feedback rather than scores; a "Moody's for Machines" rating-agency layer is unbuilt and identified as a top opening.

## Why it matters

Reputation is how a market prices trust when agents and their outputs are not interchangeable. The unbuilt rating-agency layer is named as a concrete opportunity, which is directly relevant to Godwin's interest in agentic infrastructure.
