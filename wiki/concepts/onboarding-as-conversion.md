# Onboarding as Conversion

> Treating mobile-app onboarding not as a tutorial but as a conversion sequence — a structured story that moves a new user toward paying.

## Definition

Onboarding-as-conversion frames the first-run experience of a mobile app as a deliberate persuasion arc rather than a feature walkthrough. In [[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]] it is structured as a three-act story — problem, climax (the core experience), and paywall preparation — engineered to maximize the share of users who convert to paid.

## Why it matters

Onboarding is the highest-leverage surface for conversion once traffic exists: small changes in the sequence move the paid-conversion rate. The wiki tracks it as the consumer-app counterpart of landing-page CRO and as an applied instance of the [[wiki/concepts/switching-forces]] model.

## Treatment across sources

- [[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]] frames onboarding as a three-act story (problem / climax-experience / paywall prep) and gives concrete tactics: surface an aha moment within the first minute; use questions to make users self-persuade they have a problem; mirror their answers back; run longer onboarding (10–15 min) because it converts better via loss aversion; let users use the core feature; show the review modal at peak excitement; iterate toward ≥10% conversion.

## Sub-claims and details

- Onboarding is a three-act story: problem → climax (core experience) → paywall preparation. ([[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]])
- The aha moment should land within the first minute. ([[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]])
- Onboarding questions exist to make users convince themselves they have a problem; mirror their answers back. ([[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]])
- Longer onboarding (10–15 minutes) converts better via loss aversion. ([[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]])
- Let users actually use the core feature during onboarding. ([[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]])
- Show the review modal at the peak of user excitement. ([[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]])
- Iterate the sequence until conversion reaches at least 10%. ([[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]])

## Open questions and contradictions

- The ≥10% conversion target is presented as a benchmark without segmentation by app category or price point.
