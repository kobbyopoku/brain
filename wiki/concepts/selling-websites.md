# Selling websites (AI-built local-business web agency)

> A solo-AI business path in which a practitioner builds local-business websites with AI coding tools in hours and sells them to small businesses, where the binding constraint is trust and selling, not building.

## Definition

**Selling websites** is one of five solo-AI business models catalogued in [[wiki/sources/exploraX_-5-solo-ai-business-models]]. The practitioner builds local-business sites with [[wiki/entities/claude-code|Claude Code]] in hours, then sells them to small businesses for a build fee plus a monthly retainer. The defining claim is that AI has collapsed the build cost, so the **bottleneck is trust / selling, not building**.

## Why it matters

This is the lowest-skill of the five models in the source: the technical work is largely solved by AI tooling, which shifts the entire difficulty onto distribution and sales. It is a concrete instance of the source's unifying thesis — [[distribution-as-moat]] — where the model that requires the least building most starkly exposes selling as the real constraint.

## Treatment across sources

- [[wiki/sources/exploraX_-5-solo-ai-business-models]] frames it as **Model 4**: build local-business sites with Claude Code in hours, charge $500-3k plus a $200-1k/mo retainer. The recommended stack is Google Maps (find prospects) + cold calling (close) + social media (document the work). The bottleneck is trust/selling, not building.

## Sub-claims and details

- **Build**: local-business sites built with [[wiki/entities/claude-code|Claude Code]] in hours ([[wiki/sources/exploraX_-5-solo-ai-business-models]]).
- **Pricing**: $500-3k build fee + $200-1k/mo retainer ([[wiki/sources/exploraX_-5-solo-ai-business-models]]).
- **Go-to-market stack** ([[wiki/sources/exploraX_-5-solo-ai-business-models]]):
  - **Google Maps** — source of prospects.
  - **Cold calling** — the closing channel.
  - **Social media** — document the work.
- **Bottleneck**: trust and selling, not building ([[wiki/sources/exploraX_-5-solo-ai-business-models]]).

## Open questions and contradictions

- The source treats the build as effectively free; it does not address ongoing maintenance, hosting, or how the $200-1k/mo retainer is justified to clients. Worth resolving when a primary source on the retainer mechanics is ingested.
