# Answer-engine discovery

> The shift of discovery's "first mile" into AI answer engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews) — where users learn about a product before they ever reach its homepage — which in turn changes what the homepage is for.

## Definition

**Answer-engine discovery** names the phenomenon, per [[wiki/sources/suyashkarn2-ai-trillion-dollar-blind-spot-static-website]], that the first stage of discovery is moving into AI answer engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews) ahead of any visit to a company's own site. Because the user arrives at the homepage already having "learned about you" from an answer engine, the homepage's role shifts from *learn-about-you* to *decide-whether-to-trust-you*.

It is the **destination-side counterpart** to [[ai-seo]]: AI SEO is the practitioner-side discipline of getting cited inside answer engines; answer-engine discovery is the structural fact about *where discovery now happens* that makes that discipline matter.

## Why it matters

If answer engines intercept the first mile of discovery, the static homepage no longer carries the introductory load it once did — its residual job becomes conversion and trust-verification. This reframes website strategy: optimizing the homepage to *introduce* the product is optimizing for a job that has partly moved upstream into the answer engine. The source uses this to argue that the static website is a "trillion-dollar blind spot."
