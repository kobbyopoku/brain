# Data flywheel

> A self-reinforcing loop in which every signal an AI system processes is routed back into the system so that accumulated data compounds the system's quality over time.

## Definition

A **data flywheel** is the mechanism by which an operating AI system's own outputs and observed signals feed back into it, so that each cycle of use makes the next cycle better. In the services-as-software framing of [[wiki/sources/itsalexvacca-3-phases-ai-layer]], it is what "Phase 3 (the loop)" turns into once every reply, meeting, and signal routes back into the system's spine and compounds.

## Why it matters

The flywheel is the source of accumulating, hard-to-replicate advantage in an AI-services business — it is what turns a one-time AI deployment into a compounding asset. It is the mechanism that produces a [[data-moat]].

## Treatment across sources

- [[wiki/sources/itsalexvacca-3-phases-ai-layer]] frames it as what Phase 3 (the loop) turns into: every reply/meeting/signal routes back into the spine and compounds. Quote: *"By campaign 200 the system has seen patterns no human operator could have seen at all."*

## Sub-claims and details

- The loop compounds over campaigns; by campaign 200 the system has seen patterns no human operator could have seen ([[wiki/sources/itsalexvacca-3-phases-ai-layer]]).
- Inputs to the loop are operational signals — replies, meetings, and other signals — routed back into the system's spine ([[wiki/sources/itsalexvacca-3-phases-ai-layer]]).
