# AI-native OS

> An operating system reframed so that discrete apps recede behind a single conversational AI interface that coordinates them on the user's behalf.

## Why it matters

Appears in this wiki via [[wiki/sources/shabnam-google-2026-roadmap-keynote]] as a near-term platform direction with implications for how end users reach functionality — relevant to product design that assumes app-by-app interaction.

## Treatment across sources

- [[wiki/sources/shabnam-google-2026-roadmap-keynote]] frames it as Android 17 reframed so apps become invisible behind a conversational AI interface, where a single instruction coordinates Maps, Gmail, Calendar, Payments, and Messages — "Apps become invisible. AI becomes the interface."

## Sub-claims and details

- Android 17 is presented as the vehicle for this reframing, with the OS-level AI acting as the coordination layer across first-party apps. ([[wiki/sources/shabnam-google-2026-roadmap-keynote]])

## Open questions and contradictions

- Unresolved here: how third-party (non-Google) apps participate when "the AI becomes the interface." The source does not say.
