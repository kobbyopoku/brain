# Viral Content System

> A high-volume, self-run content engine that manufactures organic reach for an app by copying proven hooks and posting across platforms at scale.

## Definition

A viral content system is a repeatable, self-made content operation designed to drive organic downloads. In [[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]] it means producing roughly 21 posts per day (7 pieces cross-posted across three platforms), copying competitor hooks, scripts, and CTAs directly, and using a small set of proven formats.

## Why it matters

It is the cheapest, most beginner-ready of the four distribution channels and a prerequisite for paid ads: the source advises having a content system working organically before spending on ads. The wiki tracks it as the organic engine inside [[wiki/concepts/mobile-app-distribution]].

## Treatment across sources

- [[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]] frames it as a self-made content engine: ~21 posts/day (7 cross-posted ×3 platforms), copying competitor hooks/scripts/CTAs directly. Formats are UGC Reaction+Demo (demo ≤8s), Slideshows (need a brand identity), and Memes. "Organic Ads" are organic-feeling hooks paired with a direct CTA; have a content system working organically before running paid ads.

## Sub-claims and details

- Target volume is ~21 posts/day — 7 pieces cross-posted across 3 platforms. ([[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]])
- Copy competitor hooks, scripts, and CTAs directly. ([[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]])
- Three formats: UGC Reaction+Demo (demo ≤8s), Slideshows (require a brand identity), and Memes. ([[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]])
- "Organic Ads" = organic-feeling hooks with a direct CTA. ([[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]])
- Have a content system working organically before running paid ads. ([[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]])

## Open questions and contradictions

- "Copy competitor hooks directly" is presented as tactical advice; the source does not address the line between modeling and infringement.
