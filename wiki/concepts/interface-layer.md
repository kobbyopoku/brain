# Interface layer (control of)

> The thesis that the decisive AI competition is over who controls the interface layer of daily life — search, assistants, operating systems, browsers, wearables, workflows — rather than over chatbots as such.

## Why it matters

Appears in this wiki via [[wiki/sources/shabnam-google-2026-roadmap-keynote]] as the load-bearing strategic claim organizing how to read big-tech AI moves — a lens relevant to platform strategy.

## Treatment across sources

- [[wiki/sources/shabnam-google-2026-roadmap-keynote]] frames it as the thread's load-bearing thesis: the real AI competition is not chatbots but "who controls the interface layer of daily life" — search, assistants, OSes, browsers, wearables, workflows.

## Sub-claims and details

- The enumerated surfaces of the interface layer are search, assistants, operating systems, browsers, wearables, and workflows. ([[wiki/sources/shabnam-google-2026-roadmap-keynote]])

## Open questions and contradictions

- Unresolved here: whether controlling the interface layer is sufficient for durable advantage, or merely necessary.
