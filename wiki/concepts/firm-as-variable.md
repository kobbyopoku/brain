# Firm as variable

> The reframing that the institutional form of the firm — not AI — is the variable, treating the hierarchical salaried corporation as a recent accident rather than the natural state of economic organization.

## Definition

"Firm as variable" is the argument that when analyzing the future of work, the unit that is changing is the firm itself, not the technology acting on it. [[wiki/sources/kushwah-aaryan-future-of-work]] states "the firm itself is the variable," framing the hierarchical salaried corporation as a roughly 60-year accident rather than the permanent or natural shape of how economic activity is organized.

## Why it matters

The reframing inverts the usual question. Instead of asking "how will AI change jobs inside firms," it asks whether the firm — as a container for salaried labor — is itself the thing being dissolved. For Godwin's interests in services-as-software and small-business automation, this treats organizational form as a design choice subject to disruption, not a fixed backdrop.

## Treatment across sources

- [[wiki/sources/kushwah-aaryan-future-of-work]] frames it as the central reframing of the essay: "the firm itself is the variable." It treats the hierarchical salaried corporation as a 60-year accident, not the natural state.

## Sub-claims and details

- The hierarchical salaried corporation is characterized as a roughly 60-year accident rather than the natural state of economic organization ([[wiki/sources/kushwah-aaryan-future-of-work]]).
- The variable under analysis is the institutional form of the firm, not AI as a technology ([[wiki/sources/kushwah-aaryan-future-of-work]]).

## Open questions and contradictions

- What follows the salaried firm as the dominant organizational form is not settled by the source as cited here.
