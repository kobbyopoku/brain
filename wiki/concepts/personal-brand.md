# Personal brand

> The public identity — bio, content, and offer — that lets an audience find and trust an individual, framed as a trust moat in an age of cheap AI-generated content.

## Definition

A personal brand is the curated public identity of an individual operating a business or audience. In [[wiki/sources/leopardracer-one-person-business-2026-ai]] it is treated as a trust moat in the age of AI and decomposed into a digital storefront/resume that sits over both content (how people find you) and offer (what you sell).

## Why it matters

In a one-person, AI-leveraged business, trust is the scarce asset; the source positions the personal brand as the moat that competitors cannot easily replicate. The wiki tracks it for its relevance to AI-services and small-business work.

## Treatment across sources

- [[wiki/sources/leopardracer-one-person-business-2026-ai]] frames the personal brand as a trust moat in the age of AI and as a digital storefront/resume over content (how people find you) and offer (what you sell). It gives a 4-step strategy: you are the niche; pick content pillars (one monetizable skill plus two complementary interests); ground the pillars in pain points, foundational topics, and high-performers; then compress to a 1–2 sentence bio.

## Open questions and contradictions

- The framework is presented as general; the source does not address how the "trust moat" framing holds up as AI-generated personal content itself proliferates.
