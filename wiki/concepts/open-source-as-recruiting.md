# Open source as recruiting

> The strategy of publishing internal tooling as open source to signal interesting problems to prospective engineers and strengthen employer brand. Appears in this wiki via [[wiki/sources/zostaff-22-hedge-fund-quant-open-source-repos]].

## Treatment across sources

- [[wiki/sources/zostaff-22-hedge-fund-quant-open-source-repos]] frames open-sourcing internal tooling as an employer-brand / talent-acquisition strategy — citing Jane Street ("not a leak, a recruiting strategy"), Two Sigma, D.E. Shaw, and HRT — where public repos and star counts signal interesting problems to prospective engineers.

## Sub-claims and details

- Per [[wiki/sources/zostaff-22-hedge-fund-quant-open-source-repos]]: Jane Street's open-source posture is characterized as "not a leak, a recruiting strategy."
- Two Sigma, D.E. Shaw, and HRT are named as firms using public repos and star counts to signal the kind of problems they work on.

## Open questions and contradictions

- Single-source; the wiki has no counter-case of a firm where open-sourcing failed as a recruiting lever.
