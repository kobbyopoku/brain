# Strategic Acquisition

> An exit to a strategic (rather than financial) acquirer, sought for higher multiples — especially for young apps — with inbound from viral content the best acquirer channel.

## Definition

A **strategic acquisition** is a sale to a buyer that acquires for strategic fit (synergy, capability, audience) rather than as a financial buyer pricing on multiples of cash flow. [[wiki/sources/mattgittleson-vibecoded-b2c-375k-exit]] argues strategic acquirers pay higher multiples, especially for young apps, and details four acquirer channels: marketplaces, brokers, outbound, and inbound — with inbound driven by viral content the best.

## Why it matters

It is the exit half of the source's playbook: the [[organic-ugc-engine]] both grows the product and generates the inbound interest that produces the best acquisition channel. Retention quality (see [[churn-at-scale]]) governs the multiple a strategic acquirer will pay, tying the engine and the exit together.

## Treatment across sources

- [[wiki/sources/mattgittleson-vibecoded-b2c-375k-exit]] frames it as arguing for strategic (vs financial) acquisitions for higher multiples, especially for young apps, and details four acquirer channels — marketplaces / brokers / outbound / inbound — with inbound from viral content being the best. Treated as a new concept candidate.

## Sub-claims and details

- Strategic acquisitions yield higher multiples than financial ones, especially for young apps. ([[wiki/sources/mattgittleson-vibecoded-b2c-375k-exit]])
- Four acquirer channels: marketplaces, brokers, outbound, inbound. ([[wiki/sources/mattgittleson-vibecoded-b2c-375k-exit]])
- Inbound from viral content is the best channel. ([[wiki/sources/mattgittleson-vibecoded-b2c-375k-exit]])
- High churn caps the multiple because acquirers discount churning MRR as not truly recurring. ([[wiki/sources/mattgittleson-vibecoded-b2c-375k-exit]]; see [[churn-at-scale]])

## Open questions and contradictions

- Single-source, candidate concept derived from one $375K exit; whether the "strategic pays more for young apps" claim generalizes is unverified in the wiki.
