# Vibe Coding

> Building software primarily by prompting AI builders, to the point where shipping an app becomes fast and easy enough that distribution, not engineering, is the bottleneck.

## Definition

Vibe coding refers to AI-assisted app development in which an AI builder handles most of the engineering, making it trivial to ship working apps in days. As used implicitly in [[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]], it is the premise — not the subject — of the source: because building has become easy, the difficulty shifts entirely onto distribution.

## Treatment across sources

- [[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]] treats vibe coding as an implicit premise: AI builders (Rork) make shipping App Store apps in days trivial, which is precisely what shifts the bottleneck onto distribution — "building got easier and distributing it didn't."

## Sub-claims and details

- AI builders make shipping App Store apps in days trivial. ([[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]])
- "Building got easier and distributing it didn't" — the bottleneck moved to distribution. ([[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]])
