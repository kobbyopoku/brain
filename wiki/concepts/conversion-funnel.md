# Conversion Funnel

> The staged path from first exposure to paid conversion, where each stage filters a fraction of the traffic that entered above it.

## Definition

A conversion funnel models a product's go-to-market as a sequence of stages, from traffic at the top to paid conversion at the bottom. In [[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]] the slogan "apps are a funnel" makes distribution the top and the paywall the bottom, with the corollary that lower-funnel optimization is wasted until the top is full.

## Why it matters

The funnel gives an ordering rule for effort: do not optimize a downstream stage before the upstream stage has volume. The wiki tracks it because it is the spine that ties [[wiki/concepts/mobile-app-distribution]] (top) to [[wiki/concepts/onboarding-as-conversion]] (bottom).

## Treatment across sources

- [[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]] frames it as "apps are a funnel" — distribution/eyeballs at the top, paid conversion at the bottom — and warns never to optimize below the top of the funnel before traffic exists; animations, retention, and even onboarding are wasted effort at low download counts.

## Sub-claims and details

- "Apps are a funnel": distribution/eyeballs at the top, paid conversion at the bottom. ([[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]])
- Never optimize below the top of the funnel before traffic exists. ([[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]])
- At low download counts, animations, retention, and even onboarding are wasted effort. ([[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]])

## Open questions and contradictions

- The "wasted effort" claim is a heuristic about sequencing; the source does not define the download threshold at which lower-funnel work begins to pay off.
