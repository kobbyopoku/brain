# Claude fluency

> Fluency in Claude is the repetition of a small fixed kit of setups across real work, not the accumulation of knowledge.

## Definition

Claude fluency, as defined by [[wiki/sources/zephyr-hg-7-setups-claude-fluency]], is reframed as a practice rather than a body of knowledge: fluency comes from repeating a small fixed kit of 7 setups across real work, not from learning more. Its slogan: "Knowledge doesn't equal fluency."

## Why it matters

The concept is a deliberate counter-frame to the wiki's many "N things to set up" onboarding sources: instead of exhaustive coverage, it argues for a minimal, repeated kit. The source encodes a 90/10 rule (90% of value sits in 7 setups) and a weekend-build estimate (~7 hours), positioning fluency as achievable in one weekend rather than 100 hours.
