# Micro-SaaS

> A solo-AI business path centered on building small, niche software tools that each solve one painful problem, billed monthly at low price points and run solo.

## Definition

**Micro-SaaS** is one of five solo-AI business models catalogued in [[wiki/sources/exploraX_-5-solo-ai-business-models]]. A micro-SaaS is a small niche tool priced at $10-49/mo that solves one painful problem, is billed monthly, and is operated by a single person. The source's central enabling claim is that [[wiki/entities/claude-code|Claude Code]] compresses what used to be 3-month builds into roughly 3 days, making it feasible to ship many small bets.

## Why it matters

Micro-SaaS is presented as the most leveraged endgame among the five models: a portfolio of small monthly-recurring tools that can compound from a few hundred dollars MRR into a $50k+/mo aggregate. It instantiates [[wiki/entities/marc-lou|Marc Lou]]'s *"ship more, attach to none"* thesis — treat each launch as a low-cost bet with a ~5% hit rate, so volume of shipping is the strategy.

## Treatment across sources

- [[wiki/sources/exploraX_-5-solo-ai-business-models]] frames it as **Model 5**: a small niche tool ($10-49/mo) solving one painful problem, billed monthly and run solo. Claude Code makes 3-month builds take 3 days. Money: $500-3k MRR as a starter target, scaling to $50k+/mo across a portfolio. Anchored on Marc Lou's *"ship more, attach to none"* / 5%-hit-rate thesis.

## Open questions and contradictions

- The source asserts a ~5% hit rate but does not specify what counts as a "hit" (any paying user? a revenue threshold?). Worth pinning down when a primary source on the portfolio economics is ingested.
