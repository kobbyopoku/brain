# Operational overhead collapse

> The argument that the operational overhead of running a business is multidimensional, that prior tooling revolutions each collapsed only one dimension, and that AI agents are the first to compress all dimensions along a single S-curve.

## Definition

Operational overhead collapse is the load-bearing original idea in [[wiki/sources/kushwah-aaryan-future-of-work]]: the overhead of running a business is not a single quantity but multidimensional. Each prior tooling revolution collapsed one dimension of that overhead; AI agents are framed as the first technology to compress all dimensions simultaneously on one S-curve. The binding constraint on this collapse is identified as "bundling," not intelligence.

## Why it matters

If overhead collapse is what makes the firm a variable, the mechanism matters: the claim that the binding constraint is bundling rather than raw intelligence reframes where the leverage is. For automation work, it implies the win is in compressing multiple overhead dimensions at once rather than improving any single capability.
