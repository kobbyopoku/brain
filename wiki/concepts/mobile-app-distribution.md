# Mobile App Distribution

> The problem of getting a mobile app in front of users — the post-vibe-coding bottleneck once building an app has become trivial.

## Definition

Mobile app distribution is the set of channels and tactics used to drive downloads of a mobile app. In the framing of [[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]], distribution is the binding constraint on app revenue: building an App Store app has become easy, so the difficulty has shifted entirely onto getting eyeballs and downloads.

## Why it matters

For a builder who can ship an app in days with an AI builder, the scarce skill is no longer engineering but distribution. The wiki tracks this because it reframes where effort and budget should go once a product exists — it cuts against the instinct to keep polishing the product.

## Treatment across sources

- [[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]] frames distribution as the post-vibe-coding bottleneck and orders four channels by beginner-readiness/budget: (1) make content yourself, (2) hire influencers, (3) hire UGC creators, (4) paid ads. It argues "apps are a funnel" and advises committing to one app until distribution is cracked, then going wide.

## Sub-claims and details

- Four distribution channels ordered by beginner-readiness and budget: make content yourself → hire influencers → hire UGC creators → paid ads. ([[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]])
- "Apps are a funnel" — distribution sits at the top of the funnel. ([[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]])
- Commit to one app until distribution is cracked, then go wide. ([[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]])
- The bottleneck shifted because "building got easier and distributing it didn't." ([[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]])

## Open questions and contradictions

- The channel ordering is presented as general guidance; the source does not quantify cost or conversion per channel beyond ordering by budget.
