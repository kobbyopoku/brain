# Competitive moat

> A business's durable source of defensibility — what stops competitors from copying it. Appears in this wiki via [[wiki/sources/zostaff-22-hedge-fund-quant-open-source-repos]], which uses the open-source question as a lens on what kind of moat a firm has.

## Treatment across sources

- [[wiki/sources/zostaff-22-hedge-fund-quant-open-source-repos]] frames it as the load-bearing thesis of the source: a firm's willingness to open-source maps onto whether its moat is *talent* (open up to recruit) or *proprietary alpha* (lock everything down). Presented as generalizable beyond quant finance to any business's defensibility question.

## Sub-claims and details

- Per [[wiki/sources/zostaff-22-hedge-fund-quant-open-source-repos]]: the open-source decision is a moat-diagnostic — firms whose edge is talent open up internal tooling to recruit; firms whose edge is proprietary alpha lock everything down.

## Open questions and contradictions

- The wiki holds only one source on this concept; the talent-vs-alpha dichotomy is asserted, not stress-tested against firms with hybrid moats.
