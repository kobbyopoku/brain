# Ruben Hassid — 3 free Claude certifications via anthropic.skilljar.com

> Practical career-signal post by [[wiki/entities/rubenhassid|Ruben Hassid]]: **only 3 Claude certifications are real and Anthropic-issued** (via `anthropic.skilljar.com`); everything else marketed as "Claude Certification" is a scam. The 3 free certs total ~6 hours: Claude 101 (1h) + AI Fluency Framework & Foundations (3h) + Introduction to Claude Cowork (2h). Cites Stanford 2025 AI Index (78% of orgs used AI in 2024, up from 55%) and PwC's *56% AI-skill wage premium* finding (up from 25%). Also surfaces [claude101.com](https://claude101.com/) — Hassid's own free Claude-guides repo.

## TL;DR

Three free official certifications:

| Cert | URL | Time | Lessons |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Claude 101** | `anthropic.skilljar.com/claude-101` | 1h | 13 lessons / 5 modules. Chat vs Cowork vs Code; Projects; Skills; Gmail/Drive/Slack connectors. |
| **AI Fluency: Framework & Foundations** | `anthropic.skilljar.com/ai-fluency-framework-foundations` | 3h | 13 lessons. The 4Ds (**D**elegation / **D**escription / **D**iscernment / **D**iligence). Effective prompting, critical thinking, ethics. *"The closest thing to taste training you'll find for free."* |
| **Introduction to Claude Cowork** | `anthropic.skilljar.com/...` | 2h | Cowork basics + Projects + Plugins + Skills + scheduled tasks + file/document handling + research at scale + permissions + model selection. |

Hassid's strong recommendation: *"If you only had time for 1 of the 3, take this one [AI Fluency]. It's the closest thing to taste training you'll find for free."*

## Key takeaways

### "Most of what's marketed as 'Claude Certification' is a scam"

> *"People are selling viral 'Claude Certifications' online right now. They are all scams. Anthropic does not endorse a single one of them. There are exactly 3 real Claude certificates I would put on LinkedIn today. All free, issued by Anthropic."*

This is the wiki's strongest "scam-warning" data point in the [[claude-code]] / Anthropic ecosystem. Worth canonizing as a fact in [[wiki/entities/anthropic]] or a [[wiki/entities/claude-certified-architect]] update.

### Stanford 2025 AI Index numbers (load-bearing)

> *"Stanford's 2025 AI Index says 78% of organizations used AI in 2024. The year before, it was 55%. That's a stupidly fast jump."*

Material adoption-rate signal. Pairs with the wiki's existing [[ai-automation-services|AI services]] thesis that the *implementation* gap matters more than the *awareness* gap — 78% of orgs are using AI but most haven't operationalized it.

### PwC AI-skill wage premium (load-bearing)

> *"Workers with AI skills get a 56% wage premium on average. Last year, it was 25%."*

Adoption-economics signal. Year-over-year 25% → 56% premium expansion is the kind of compounding signal that strengthens [[wiki/concepts/horizontal-leverage|horizontal-leverage]] (sharpener vs hollower). Worth flagging in [[cognitive-debt]] and [[horizontal-leverage]] cross-cite — the *premium* is the empirical face of the sharpener-hollower split that Rohit and Saraev articulate theoretically.

### LinkedIn signal: "less than 2% of the world has actually touched the tool"

Hassid's framing for *why* a cert matters even if it doesn't get you hired alone: it puts you in the small fraction of the workforce that has demonstrably used the tool. Combined with the 56% wage premium, it's a low-cost asymmetric career bet.

### claude101.com (Hassid's own repo)

> *"PS: I made my own version of claude101 → claude101.com. Claude101 is a repo of all of my favorite Claude guides. Only the free ones."*

Free curated Claude-guides aggregator. Worth flagging in [[wiki/entities/anthropic-skills|anthropic-skills]] / [[claude-code]] as an external resource collection peer to the official cert path.
