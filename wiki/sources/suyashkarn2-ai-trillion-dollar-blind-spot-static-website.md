# SuyashKarn2 — AI's Trillion-Dollar Blind Spot: The Static Website

> Founder thesis (and product pitch for [[wiki/entities/interact-labs|Interact Labs]]) arguing that while every company races to embed AI *inside* its products, the customer-facing front door — the static website — remains an unsolved, trillion-dollar "blind spot" that should be rebuilt as a conversational interface that assembles itself around each visitor.

## TL;DR

SuyashKarn2 argues the static marketing website is a structural relic: a single page can't adapt to context, answer follow-ups, or tell what matters to one visitor versus another, so companies waste effort on copy tweaks, A/B tests, and bolted-on chatbots that don't fix the underlying *interface* problem. Meanwhile the "first mile of discovery" is being absorbed by AI answer engines ([[wiki/entities/chatgpt|ChatGPT]], [[wiki/entities/perplexity-ai|Perplexity]], Google AI Overviews), so by the time a prospect reaches a homepage they're verifying a conclusion, not discovering. The proposed replacement is not a better page but a different interface entirely — one that converses, adapts, and "assembles itself around the person in real time," grounded in the company's actual data. The piece closes by framing the broader target as *every* company-to-customer interface (onboarding, docs, support, sales enablement, in-product education) as "the interface layer," pitched as bigger than search or social. The author's company, [[wiki/entities/interact-labs|Interact Labs]] (interactlabs.ai), claims to be building it.

## Key takeaways

- **The blind spot framing**: every major company is racing to embed AI *inward* (smarter search, faster onboarding, automated support), yet the first thing customers see is still "a static page of text and graphics built for an older internet" — the front door hasn't changed even though user expectations have.
- **Static websites are an interface problem, not a copy/conversion problem**: a single page must speak to every audience (founders, enterprise buyers, engineers, operators, executives, end users) and cannot adapt to context or answer follow-ups. Rewriting copy, swapping headlines, A/B testing button colours, and adding a corner chatbot are compensations, not fixes.
- **Conversation is how people assess relevance**: "static pages cannot have conversations," and conversation + exploration is how visitors actually decide whether a product solves *their* problem (Is this for a company like mine? Will it work with my stack?).
- **The "book a call" CTA is high friction**: only a tiny minority book, because it's a big ask for someone still unsure the product meets their requirements.
- **The first mile of discovery is being absorbed by AI answer engines**: people increasingly ask [[wiki/entities/chatgpt|ChatGPT]], [[wiki/entities/perplexity-ai|Perplexity]], or Google's AI-generated results *before* visiting a homepage. ([[wiki/concepts/ai-seo|AI-SEO]] connection.)
- **The homepage's role is shifting from learn-about-you to decide-whether-to-trust-you**: "slowly, and then suddenly" the homepage stops being where people learn about you and becomes where they decide whether to trust you.
- **Google I/O 2026 cited as accelerant**: Google "doubled down on AI Overviews in Search," pushing to answer queries directly before users click through to any website.
- **The proposed replacement is a generative, conversational interface**: not a static page with an AI widget bolted on the corner, but an interface that "converses, adapts, and assembles itself around the person in real time."
- **Audience-calibrated, on-the-fly views**: a founder gets a view calibrated to their stage and risk profile; a CISO gets a controls framework; an engineer describing their stack gets a live integration map — "grounded in the company's actual data, specific to them."
- **"The conversation is the website"** — not a conversation layered on top of the website.
- **It doubles as a real-time intent/objection sensor for sales**: the interface surfaces user intent, objections, priorities, and buying signals, so by the time a prospect is ready to buy, sales already knows what matters — framed as a strategic advantage, not just better conversion data.
- **The landing page is "just the wedge"**: the real target is *every* interface where a company meets a customer — onboarding, documentation, support, sales enablement, in-product education — all currently static and generic.
- **"That's not a market segment. That's the market."** — the interface layer between companies and customers is pitched as one of the largest surfaces in software, "bigger than search, bigger than social," with almost nobody building for it.
- **The source is simultaneously a thesis and a product pitch**: it closes with a CTA to try [[wiki/entities/interact-labs|interactlabs.ai]] ("Click 'Interact Now' — best experienced on a desktop or laptop").
- **Historical anchor**: the thread includes an image captioned "Salesforce website in 1995" to dramatize how little the website-as-interface has structurally changed.

## Notes

- **Genre is founder-thesis-as-pitch.** The argument is rhetorically strong but the supporting evidence is assertion, not data — there are no conversion figures, A/B results, or independent benchmarks for the claimed superiority of a "generative website." Treat the trillion-dollar / "bigger than search" sizing as marketing framing, not a substantiated TAM. The named beneficiary is the author's own product, [[wiki/entities/interact-labs|Interact Labs]].
- **The strongest, most defensible claim is the answer-engine displacement of discovery** — that AI Overviews / ChatGPT / Perplexity intercept queries before the homepage. This is the same structural shift the wiki already tracks under [[wiki/concepts/ai-seo|AI-SEO]] (optimizing to appear in AI-generated answers), but viewed from the *destination* side: if discovery happens in the answer engine, the website's residual job changes from "explain" to "convert/verify trust."
- **Tension with the wiki's existing landing-page material.** [[wiki/sources/clear_graphics-yc-unicorn-landing-pages|clear_graphics' YC-unicorn analysis]] catalogs 11 patterns that make *static* landing pages convert; this source argues the static page is the wrong unit entirely. They aren't strictly contradictory — the YC patterns optimize the current artifact; SuyashKarn2 argues for replacing the artifact — but they represent opposing bets on whether the static page is worth optimizing. Worth a future synthesis on "optimize the page vs. replace the page."
- **Relevance to Godwin's products.** The "audience-calibrated, generated-on-the-fly view grounded in the company's actual data" concept maps directly onto a generative-UI surface for commercial products like [[wiki/projects/vedge|Vedge]] / [[wiki/projects/kivora|Kivora]] / [[wiki/projects/clarvyn|Clarvyn]] marketing sites — a CISO-gets-a-controls-framework view is exactly a GRC-product pitch. The intent/objection-sensing-for-sales angle is also a concrete services play. Flagged as a thesis worth pressure-testing rather than an established pattern.
- **Uncertainty**: `source_date` (2026-05-20) is taken from the raw frontmatter's `published` field and a "Google I/O yesterday" reference; the raw `created` was 2026-05-21. The X status-id (2057099123413946617) is preserved in `source_url`. The "Salesforce website in 1995" caption is the author's, not independently verified here.
