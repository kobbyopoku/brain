# Pascal — Hantavirus profit thread (ethically dark; ingested as negative example)

> **Ethical flag**: this post advocates exploiting a hypothetical hantavirus outbreak for profit, citing four COVID-2020-derived "playbook" conditions (captive attention, acute pain, forced adoption, low fulfillment cost). Ingested for completeness — the wiki records what was said, not what is endorsed. **Worth keeping in the wiki as a negative example of where unconstrained AI-automation-services thinking can lead when divorced from ethics.**

## TL;DR

Pascal argues that *"Hantavirus is going to make a lot of people rich"* and recommends 5 tactics to capitalize on a potential pandemic-driven market disruption:

1. Raising prices significantly on existing offers.
2. Pre-selling non-existent products.
3. Running paid advertising campaigns at lower costs.
4. Launching high-ticket offerings.
5. Acquiring assets from burned-out creators.

The post acknowledges *"people are dying"* but the framing remains profit-focused throughout, advocating *"doing something useful with the upside"* and warning that *"most people will not run these plays"* — i.e. positioning early actors at advantage.

## Why this is ingested honestly with an ethical flag

- **Pre-selling non-existent products** during a public health crisis is fraud-adjacent.
- **Acquiring assets from burned-out creators** during a crisis is opportunistic in ways most ethical frameworks would object to.
- **Raising prices on existing offers** during widespread economic distress is the canonical "price gouging" complaint.
