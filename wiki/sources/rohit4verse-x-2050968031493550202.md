# Rohit — There is no neutral way to use AI (cognitive debt; 5 protocols)

> Rohit's substantive thesis: *"There is no neutral way to use AI. You either get sharper using it or you get hollower."* Coins **cognitive debt** as the failure mode where AI-augmented work erodes underlying competence over time. Cites an MIT Media Lab study showing weakened neural connectivity in students using ChatGPT — and reversal when groups swapped tools. Worth promoting **cognitive-debt** to a first-class wiki concept.

## TL;DR

Two categories of AI users emerging:

- **Hollowers**: outsource thinking before building competence. AI-explanation replaces actual engagement. Result: can't function without AI; can't read your own AI-written code weeks later.
- **Sharpeners**: think first, use AI to amplify. AI-explanation comes *after* independent cognitive engagement. Result: more valuable over time; AI extends the reach of real competence.

Rohit's distinction: *"Using AI before independent cognitive engagement builds debt. Using AI after independent cognitive engagement amplifies you."*

## Key takeaways

### The MIT Media Lab study (cited)

Students using ChatGPT showed **weakened neural connectivity** during the experiment. When groups swapped tools in session four:
- **Hand-writers who then gained AI access**: showed *enhanced* cognitive engagement.
- **AI-dependent users who lost AI access**: couldn't function without it.

The asymmetry is the core insight — same tool, different outcome based on *order of use*. AI-after-thinking is generative; AI-before-thinking is debilitating.

### Five protocols for sharpening (not hollowing)

1. **Think first, prompt second** — spend 10 minutes generating rough answers before using AI.
2. **Force disagreement** — ask models to critique your weakest claims rather than confirm your strongest.
3. **Reverse the flow** — explain concepts to AI rather than learning from it.
4. **Verify load-bearing claims** — through primary sources, not via the model's confidence.
5. **Final synthesis with AI closed** — write the conclusion from memory.

These are **complementary to existing wiki concepts**:
- Protocol 4 ("verify load-bearing claims") matches the wiki's own *cite-or-omit* rule in [[CLAUDE]].
- Protocol 3 ("reverse the flow") is what this brain-as-LLM-Wiki does — the human writes; the LLM organizes; the human re-reads.
- Protocol 1 ("think first, prompt second") is the discipline behind `superpowers:brainstorming`-style skill workflows.

### The category distinction matters

Rohit's framing makes a structural argument: **two categories of users are emerging**, and they will diverge in market value over time. Those using AI to *do existing work easier* (hollowers) become replaceable; those using AI to *attempt harder problems* (sharpeners) become more valuable.

Worth pairing with [[wiki/concepts/horizontal-leverage|Saraev's horizontal-leverage thesis]]: Saraev predicts wealth concentration in the AI-amplified-employee niche; Rohit predicts the *which-employees* answer is determined by cognitive-debt vs cognitive-amplification orientation.
