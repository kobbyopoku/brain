# Greg Isenberg — The 36 biggest startup opportunities right now

> A category-by-category market map of 36 startup opportunities, organized one-per-vertical, where the through-line is that most of the biggest opportunities are *reactions to AI* (loneliness, verification, belonging, analog) rather than AI itself — with #2 ("managed AI employees for businesses") sitting directly on the [[wiki/concepts/ai-automation-services|AI-services]] thesis the wiki owner builds against.

## TL;DR

Greg Isenberg lists 36 startup opportunities, each tagged "biggest [category]" — one opportunity per vertical (b2c, b2b, edtech, SaaS, gaming, dating, etc.). The single load-bearing claim is the framing, not any individual entry: a large share of the named opportunities are **counter-positions against AI saturation** — loneliness, third spaces, human verification, analog products, belonging, IRL experiences — while another cluster is **agentic AI applied to a vertical** (managed AI employees, agentic commerce, accounting agents, autonomous travel rebooking). Top picks: biggest b2c = solving loneliness; biggest b2b = **managed AI employees for businesses** (#2). A top reply from commenter Thomas Power crystallizes the framing: the deeper pattern is that these are reactions to AI rather than AI itself.

## Key takeaways

- The list is a **market map, not a deep analysis** — one line per opportunity, organized as "biggest [category]." Its value is breadth and the cross-cutting pattern, not depth on any single idea.
- **#1 biggest b2c: solving loneliness** — third spaces, community apps, IRL gatherings.
- **#2 biggest b2b: managed AI employees for businesses** — Isenberg's pick for the single biggest b2b opportunity; directly aligned with the wiki's [[wiki/concepts/ai-automation-services|AI-automation-services]] and [[wiki/concepts/services-as-software|services-as-software]] theses. (See Notes on the owner-relevance and the Howard Lerman / "Roam" reply.)
- **#10 biggest SaaS: pay-per-outcome pricing** — restates the outcome-based / pay-per-outcome pricing thesis the wiki tracks under [[wiki/concepts/services-as-software]].
- **#12 biggest talent: training non-technical people to operate agents** — an explicit "operator economy" call; humans-as-agent-operators as a market.
- **#17 biggest anti-slop play: digital verification that you're a real human** — Isenberg predicts "every platform will need this within 2 years"; the human-verification opportunity overlaps conceptually with [[wiki/entities/worldcoin|Worldcoin]]'s proof-of-personhood thesis.
- **#18 biggest infrastructure: agent permissions, security, audit trails** — agent-governance infrastructure as a standalone opportunity; adjacent to the GRC/compliance domain the owner works in.
- **#7 biggest ecommerce / #29 biggest dating: agentic intermediation** — agents that shop and buy for you (agentic commerce) and agent-mediated matchmaking; agent-as-actor patterns applied to consumer verticals.
- **A large cluster of entries are reactions to AI, not AI itself**: loneliness (#1), small social / group-chats-as-products (#6), anti-screen offline kits (#13), spiritual belonging (#14), human matchmaking (#29), and analog/nostalgia products (#36, "counter-positioning against AI everything").
- **A second cluster is "agentic AI applied to vertical X"**: managed AI employees (#2), AI service advisor for dealerships (#11), bookkeeping agents charging per transaction (#21), family ops automation (#20), autonomous trip planning/rebooking (#31), AI tutors that adapt through conversation (#9).
- **#27 biggest regulated: on-device / local AI** — "healthcare, legal, finance open up when data stays local"; the data-residency argument relevant to PHI-bearing systems like [[wiki/projects/vedge|Vedge]].
- **#3 elder tech, #15 longevity biomarkers, #24 at-home skincare diagnostics, #32 blood-work-based nutrition, #33 pet health monitoring** — a health/longevity/diagnostics cluster framed around consumer self-management.
- **#35 biggest robotics: physical AI, "$30 brains on existing hardware"** — cheap intelligence retrofitted onto existing physical hardware rather than new robots.
- The post is a high-engagement X thread; replies include Howard Lerman, Joseph Miclaus, Thomas Power, and Nick Vasilescu, with Isenberg responding to several.

## Notes

- **Owner relevance is concentrated in #2.** "Managed AI employees for businesses" is the exact category the wiki owner operates in via [[wiki/entities/roam-labs|ROAM Labs]]'s services arm and is the conceptual core of [[wiki/concepts/ai-automation-services]] and [[wiki/concepts/services-as-software]]. Isenberg naming it the single biggest b2b opportunity is corroborating market signal for that thesis, from a high-reach source.
- **Name-collision flag — "Roam" in the replies is NOT the owner's ROAM Labs.** Commenter Howard Lerman (@howard) writes *"Roam's about to ship #2 cc @shelbyleim"*; Howard Lerman is the founder of Roam (the virtual-HQ / remote-office company) and Yext, and this reply refers to *his* company, not [[wiki/entities/roam-labs|ROAM Labs]]. These are unrelated entities that happen to share a name. The raw source does not state which Roam; this attribution to Howard Lerman's Roam is inferred from the @howard handle and is uncertain — do not conflate with ROAM Labs.
- **The list is assertion, not evidence.** No market-sizing, no validation methodology, no sources behind the "biggest" claims beyond Isenberg's own read (the one numeric figures present — "70 million boomers" for #3, "$140B" pet industry for #33, "$30 brains" for #35 — are stated without citation). Treat as a trend-thesis / idea-prompt artifact, not analysis.
- **Structural quirk in the raw:** entries #4 and #16 are duplicates ("biggest mobile: action apps that do things, not apps you stare at"), so the list effectively names 35 distinct opportunities under 36 numbered slots.
- **Thomas Power's reply is arguably the most analytically valuable content in the thread** — it abstracts the 36 entries into a single pattern (opportunities-as-reactions-to-AI). That pattern is the reason this source earns wiki-shelf-space beyond a generic listicle.
- **@shelbyleim** is cc'd by Howard Lerman but not otherwise identified in the source; insufficient citable facts to warrant an entity page.
