# Alex Vacca — ColdIQ AI-native services-as-software (3 workflows)

> Alex Vacca's tactical follow-up to [[wiki/sources/itsalexvacca-services-as-software-7m-agency|his foundational $7M-ARR services-as-software playbook]]. Documents the **3 specific operational workflows** ColdIQ uses to compress per-employee throughput by ~3-4×: list-building (Lovable-built tool), copywriting (Twain + Clay integration), and campaign deployment (Claude Code on Instantly). One GTM engineer went from 4 active clients to 13 without quality degradation. Operator-window-closing claim recurs.

## TL;DR

> *"Per-employee throughput inside the delivery org compresses by a factor of three or four."*

The model: sell *services* (not software) but deliver *AI-native* — strategic decisions stay human-driven; the production layer underneath collapses into AI automation.

## The 3 workflows

### Workflow 1 — List building

- **Built on**: [Lovable](https://lovable.dev) (vibe-coded internal tool).
- **Replaces**: manual list creation across multiple platforms.
- **Interface**: operators give plain-language ICP queries; system filters and enriches prospects automatically.
- **Compression target**: time-to-list from hours to minutes.

### Workflow 2 — Copywriting

- **Built on**: [Twain](https://twainai.com) integration within [Clay](https://clay.com).
- **Output**: personalized email sequences.
- **Discipline**: strategic decisions remain human-driven; *"the production layer underneath is what collapses"* into AI automation.

### Workflow 3 — Campaign deployment

- **Built on**: [[wiki/entities/claude-code|Claude Code]] driving [Instantly](https://instantly.ai).
- **Replaces**: manual list-uploading, variable-mapping, send-configuration.
- **Per-campaign cost**: 1-2 hours of human work eliminated.

## Key takeaways

### Cost-compression arithmetic

- **Before**: 1 GTM engineer manages 4 active clients.
- **After**: same engineer manages 13 clients without quality degradation.
- **Throughput**: ~3.25× per-employee.
- **Revenue per team member**: nearly tripled.
- **Cost per client**: held flat.

This is the **operational instantiation** of the [[services-as-software]] thesis Vacca already canonized in the wiki. The earlier source covered the strategic argument; this thread covers the *what specifically did we automate* operational details.

### Operator window closing

Vacca's recurring framing: *"the operator window is closing as the category gains mainstream attention."* The 18-month window claim from [[ai-automation-services]] (Khairallah / Saraev / heynavtoor) recurs with Vacca's framing — strengthens cross-source agreement on the time-bound nature of the opportunity.

### Strategic-vs-production layer split

The most architecturally interesting point: *"strategic decisions remain human-driven; the production layer underneath collapses into AI automation."* This is the [[reasoning-execution-split]] principle expressed in services-business terms: humans reason about *who to target / what to say / how to position*; AI executes *list-building / copy-generation / campaign-deployment*.
