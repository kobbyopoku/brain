# The Truth About Making Money Online in 2026 (And What's About to Die) — Brian Moran

> A 15-year-veteran's catalog of 10 online business models that work in 2026 plus 1 that's collapsing. Notable for the dominant thesis: as AI commoditizes information, the human-touch premium grows — and recurring monthly subscriptions only survive when products are used daily.

## TL;DR

Brian Moran (founder of [[wiki/entities/samcart]], $6B+ in processed sales for 100k+ entrepreneurs) argues that the $20-200/month community model is dying — churn destroys it within months — and walks through 10 models that still work in 2026: high-ticket coaching, low-ticket digital products as customer-acquisition, yearly newsletters at ~$100/year, supplements/physical products, build-your-own-apps via [[wiki/entities/claude-code|Claude Code]] / [[wiki/entities/cursor]] / [[wiki/entities/replit]], in-person events, exclusive $5k+/year communities, daily-use software/tools (the only viable recurring model), investing/financial advice, and AI services for businesses ($5-20k/project). Recurrent thesis: as AI commoditizes information, human-touch and human-to-human delivery accrue premium pricing.

## Key takeaways

- **The dying model**: monthly $20-$200 communities. Churn at scale destroys them; most members pay for something they don't use even once a month. Recurring billing only works for daily-use products.
- **10 models that still work in 2026**:
  1. **High-ticket coaching ($2K-$10K+)** — human touch is the scarcity. AI gives generic advice; a coach who's been where you're trying to go and looks at your specific situation gets $5K+.
  2. **Low-ticket digital products ($7-$97)** — not the business; the **customer acquisition layer** for the high-ticket backend. A customer is worth 10× a lead.
  3. **Newsletters with yearly billing (~$100/year)** — Agora-style. Yearly removes the monthly cancel-decision; "$100/year for valuable content" is an impulse buy that survives.
  4. **Supplements and physical products** — AI-resistant. Top SamCart sellers right now. "Diversify before you're forced to."
  5. **Build-your-own-apps via AI** — [[wiki/entities/claude-code|Claude Code]], [[wiki/entities/cursor]], [[wiki/entities/replit]] mean digital product sellers can ship apps instead of PDFs. Author claims he built an iOS app in an hour that engineering said would take months. Cross-cuts with [[ai-automation-services]].
  6. **In-person events ($2K-$5K tickets)** — high-touch, AI-resistant; create superfans via testimonials and content reuse.
  7. **Exclusive high-ticket communities ($5K+/year)** — small group, high engagement, exclusivity, status purchase. *"Don't try to get 5,000 members at $50/month. Get 50 members at $5,000/year."*
  8. **Software and tools — daily use only** — the only recurring model the author would start. *"If people use it once a month or less, they will cancel. It's just a matter of time."*
  9. **Investing / financial advice** — $2K-$5K access offers; "people will always pay for help with their money."
  10. **AI services for businesses ($5K-$20K/project)** — see [[ai-automation-services]] and [[services-as-software]]. The wiki's other ingested sources ([[wiki/sources/khairallah-ai-automations-10k-month]], [[wiki/sources/itsalexvacca-services-as-software-7m-agency]]) cover this in much more detail.
- **Master thesis**: human touch becomes more valuable as AI commoditizes information. Charge for experience, judgment, and presence — not for facts.
- **Author's pattern recognition** is built on real SamCart data — what's at the top of conversion is supplements, high-ticket coaching, financial newsletters, and AI services.

## Notable quotes

> "AI is changing everything... pure information is becoming free, and that's going to hurt a lot of digital product sellers who are basically just selling information."

> "The only time recurring works is if you have a tool or piece of software people use every single day."

> "If you have expertise and you're not offering something at the $2K+ level, you're leaving serious money on the table."

> "Don't try to get 5,000 members at $50/month. Work hard to get 50 members at $5,000/year."

> "If you can build AI workflows, automations, and little apps for businesses that are lagging behind, you can charge premium prices."

## Notes

- **This source triangulates with [[wiki/sources/khairallah-ai-automations-10k-month]] and [[wiki/sources/itsalexvacca-services-as-software-7m-agency]]** on the AI-services-for-businesses opportunity. Three sources, three vintages, same conclusion: there's a $5-20k/project market with more demand than supply *right now*. Worth tracking how this concept ages.
- **The "monthly community is dead" claim** is a useful counter-narrative to the prevailing creator-economy SaaS-community pitch. Worth carrying as a contrasting position when later sources advocate community as a moat.
- **Author's credibility signals** are SamCart's $6B in processed sales and the 100k+ entrepreneurs metric. Both are author-claimed; neither is independently verified here. Treat as the author's prior.
- **Build-your-own-apps point (#5)** quietly assumes the [[claude-code-skills]] / [[wiki/entities/claude-code]] toolchain. The wiki's tooling sources cover the *how*; this source covers the *commercial implication*.
- **The author argues for diversification away from pure-information products before AI forces it** — a strategic argument with a concrete window. Worth re-reading in 12 months.
