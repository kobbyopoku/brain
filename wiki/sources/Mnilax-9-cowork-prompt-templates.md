# Mnilax — 9 Cowork prompt-templates running an 8hr workday in 47min of supervision

> Mnilax (existing wiki entity — 430-hours-instrumented-study author) ships **9 Claude Cowork slash commands** that pull back 34 hours/week of his workload. *"Forty-seven minutes — that's the median amount of time my hands are on the keyboard during what used to be a full workday. The eight hours of actual task time still happens. It just happens in the background while nine Cowork prompt-templates do the work and ping me when they need a decision."* Methodology: 30 days of paired before/after timing in May 2026. Failure rate documented: started with ~30 templates; **21 didn't survive**, 9 did.

## The mental model — slash command = recipe, plugin = kitchen

> *"Most public Cowork content is about which plugin to install. That's the wrong layer. The plugin is the kitchen. The slash command is the recipe. You can buy every kitchen on the market and still cook nothing without recipes."*

A Cowork slash command is a *saved prompt with structured inputs*. Type `/morning-brief`, Cowork pops a form with the prompt's fields, you fill them in, walk away.

This is the strongest articulation in the wiki of *plugin vs slash-command discipline*. Aligns with [[wiki/concepts/markdown-as-agent-contract|markdown-as-agent-contract]]: the slash command IS the contract; the plugin is the runtime.
