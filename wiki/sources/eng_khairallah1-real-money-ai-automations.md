# How to Make Real Money Building AI Automations for Projects (Full Course) — Khairallah

> A beginner-oriented 7-step playbook for selling AI automations to small businesses — pick a niche, learn the workflow, build a 5-minute proof-of-concept demo, package three tiered offers ($500-$5,000 + $1-3k/mo retainer), land the first three clients via cold outreach, then scale through niche-specific template libraries. Khairallah's second AI-automation-services course in the wiki, pitched at non-engineers.

## TL;DR

Khairallah argues a "gold rush" exists in the gap between small businesses knowing AI exists and actually using it to solve their problems, and that filling it requires no software-engineering background — only understanding [[wiki/entities/claude-code|Claude]], building basic automations, and explaining value in plain English. The piece is a seven-step operational walkthrough: pick one niche, learn its workflow by interviewing 3-5 people, build a single proof-of-concept demo, package three tiered offers, land your first three clients through direct outreach/network/free demos, deliver and document, then scale with niche-specific templates. It closes with five concrete "build this week" automations and a month-by-month first-year revenue ramp ($1,500-$3,000 in months 1-2 to $8,000-$20,000+/mo by months 7-12). Pitched explicitly at non-engineers as a real (not passive) service business with a closing window.

## Key takeaways

- **Market thesis**: small businesses have the same operational problems as large companies but none of the resources to solve them; the gap "between 'AI exists' and 'AI is solving my problems' is where the money is right now." Builders charge **$500-$5,000 per automation** and some run **$2,000-$10,000/mo recurring**; most found their first three clients via cold outreach or word of mouth.
- **No engineering background required** — the stated prerequisites are understanding Claude, building basic automations, and explaining value in plain English. This is the most explicitly *non-technical* framing of the [[ai-automation-services]] model in the wiki.
- **Concrete time-cost examples per niche**: law firm (5 attorneys) spends 10 hrs/week organizing/summarizing client documents; real estate agency 8 hrs/week on property descriptions + market analyses; marketing agency 15 hrs/week on content first drafts; accounting firm 12 hrs/week on expense reports.
- **Step 1 — Pick a niche**: don't serve "small businesses" broadly. Named best niches: real estate agencies, law firms, marketing agencies, e-commerce, accounting firms, recruiting agencies. Pick where you have existing knowledge or connections for credibility.
- **Step 2 — Learn the workflow**: interview 3-5 people in the niche; look for tasks that are **repetitive + pattern-based + time-consuming (30+ min) + low-complexity**. A task hitting all four criteria is a potential automation.
- **Step 3 — Build a proof of concept**: take the single most impactful task and build one working demo. The real-estate example: feed a raw MLS listing data sheet to Claude with a luxury-copywriter [[markdown-as-agent-contract|system prompt]], show raw-data-in / polished-listing-out. "That five-minute demo is worth more than any sales pitch you could ever write."
- **Step 4 — Package three tiered offers** (sell outcomes, not "AI consulting"):
  - **Package 1 — The Quick Win ($500-$1,000)**: one automation for one task, delivered as a documented Claude workflow (system prompt + context document + instructions). Timeline 1-2 days.
  - **Package 2 — The System Build ($2,000-$5,000)**: 3-5 automations covering a complete workflow area via a [[wiki/entities/cowork|Claude Cowork]] setup or set of [[wiki/entities/cowork|Claude Code routines]], intake-to-output, with team training. Timeline 1-2 weeks.
  - **Package 3 — The Managed Service ($1,000-$3,000/mo)**: ongoing build + management + updates + troubleshooting. "This is where recurring revenue lives." Monthly reporting on time saved and outputs produced.
  - Sequencing: start with Package 1 to land first clients, graduate to Package 2 once you have case studies, add Package 3 once relationships are ongoing.
- **Step 5 — Find first three clients** without a website, funnel, or audience — "you need three conversations." Three methods: (1) **Direct outreach** on Google Maps / LinkedIn / local directories with a specific time-saving message; (2) **existing network** ("one referral is worth ten cold messages"); (3) **free demonstration** for the first client to generate a case study. Goal: three paying clients within 30 days.
- **Step 6 — Deliver and document** (7 sub-steps): interview (30 min) → build (2-4 hrs basic workflow) → test with real data (1-2 hrs) → iterate 1-2 rounds → document → 30-min training walkthrough → one-week follow-up. Total 5-10 hrs for a $500-$1,000 project = **$50-$200/hr** of the builder's time.
- **Step 7 — Scale with templates**: every automation built for one client in a niche adapts to other clients in the same niche with light tweaks. "Build once. Sell many times." After five clients in a niche, a template library delivers a $2,000 project in one-third the original time — "that is how margins grow."
- **Five automations to build and sell this week**: (1) **Client Onboarding Processor** — intake forms → structured profile + welcome email, saves 45 min/client; (2) **Weekly Report Generator** — multi-source data → formatted weekly summary, saves 2-3 hrs/week/account; (3) **Content Repurposer** — long-form → 5 social posts + 3 subject lines + LinkedIn summary, saves 90 min/piece; (4) **Review Response Drafter** — monitors Google/Yelp reviews → tone-matched drafts, saves 4-5 hrs/week; (5) **Proposal Generator** — sales-call notes → structured proposal with scope/timeline/pricing, saves 2-3 hrs/proposal.
- **First-year revenue ramp** (author's model, framed as "what people in the AI automation space are actually reporting right now in May 2026"): Months 1-2 — 3 clients @ $500-$1,000 = $1,500-$3,000 ("you are learning"); Months 3-4 — 3-5 clients @ $1,000-$2,000 = $3,000-$10,000 (case studies); Months 5-6 — system builds $3,000-$5,000 + managed services $1,500-$2,500/mo = $5,000-$15,000/mo; Months 7-12 — recurring + faster delivery = $8,000-$20,000+/mo.
- **Window argument**: the barrier to entry is still low because most small businesses have never been pitched an AI automation service ("a category that did not exist 18 months ago"); the window will close as competition increases and prices normalize. Early movers get a head start.

## Notes

- **This is Khairallah's second AI-automation-services course in the wiki** and a deliberately *lighter, more beginner-facing* sibling to [[wiki/sources/khairallah-ai-automations-10k-month]]. Differences worth tracking:
  - **Technical depth**: the $10K/mo source front-loads a four-skill technical toolkit (context files, skill files, [[mcp-server|MCP server]] wiring, [[scheduled-automation|scheduled automations]]) and explicitly names MCP servers ([[wiki/entities/tavily|Tavily]] / [[wiki/entities/google-drive|Drive]] / [[wiki/entities/gmail|Gmail]] / [[wiki/entities/slack|Slack]]). This source omits MCP and skill-file architecture entirely — its build unit is "a Claude workflow with a system prompt and context document." It is the non-engineer's on-ramp.
  - **Pricing floor is lower**: $500 entry (Quick Win) vs. the $3,000-$15,000 build range in the $10K/mo source. The two together describe a *price ladder* — start clients cheap to land them, graduate to the higher-ticket System Builds.
  - **Niches mostly overlap** (real estate, law firms, marketing agencies, e-commerce, accounting) but this source adds **recruiting agencies** and drops healthcare/financial-advisors. Recruiting is a net-new niche for the wiki's niche prior.
- **Cold-outreach method overlaps with [[wiki/sources/exploraX_-google-maps-leadgen|m0h's Google Maps lead-gen]]** — both name Google Maps as the prospect-discovery surface. This source's outreach script is lighter (one template) vs. m0h's structured 4-signal scoring; the two pair as low-effort vs. systematic versions of the same first-clients problem.
- **Cowork + Routines named as the Package-2 delivery substrate** — consistent with Khairallah's other wiki source on [[wiki/sources/eng_khairallah1-x-2052684086414852546|three-session Cowork daily architecture]]. The author is consistently a Cowork-first (vs. raw Claude Code) advocate.
- **All pricing and revenue figures are the author's model, not verified market data** — the month-by-month ramp ($1,500/mo → $20,000+/mo) is asserted, hedged only as "what people in the AI automation space are actually reporting." Same unverifiability caveat as the $10K/mo source; treat as strategic prior.
- **The "18 months / window closing" claim recurs** across both Khairallah sources — a stable strategic thesis from this author, not a one-off. Worth a [[lint]] flag if a late-2026 source confirms or contradicts saturation.
- **Proof-of-concept-as-sales-tool is the sharpest contribution** of this source vs. its sibling: the "five-minute demo" reframes selling from pitching to *showing*, which is a distinct tactic the $10K/mo source's free-case-study phase only partially captures.
