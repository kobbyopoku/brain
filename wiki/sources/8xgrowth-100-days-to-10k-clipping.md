# 8xgrowth — "What I'd do if I lost everything and had 100 days to get back to $10k"

> A 5-phase, 100-day "restart from zero to $10k/month" execution plan built around UGC **content-clipping reward campaigns** plus AI agents (Content Rewards + Adaptive) that reverse-engineer viral short-form video — the consumer-content-economy cousin of the wiki's [[wiki/concepts/ai-automation-services|AI automation services]] playbooks.

## TL;DR

8xgrowth lays out a day-by-day blueprint to rebuild a $10k/month income in 100 days using exactly two tools: [[wiki/entities/content-rewards|Content Rewards]] (a paid-per-view clipping campaign marketplace) for the earning side, and [[wiki/entities/adaptive-ai|Adaptive]] for AI automation. The thesis is **systems over luck**: instead of "feeling" the algorithm, build AI agents that scrape and decode the structure of viral YouTube Shorts, reverse-engineer the fastest-growing channels' playbooks, study outliers (viral hits vs. flops), and auto-post finished clips from Google Drive to socials. The arc runs setup (Week 1) → automation build (Week 2) → grind/100+ clips (Weeks 3-4, ~$1k-$3k) → scaling with 5 more accounts + hired editors (Month 2) → $10k milestone by Day 100. This is a promotional thread (engagement-gated DM for tool links).

## Key takeaways

- The plan targets **$10k/month in 100 days from zero**, framed as "lost everything" — a restart-from-scratch narrative aimed at creators/operators.
- Only **two tools** are required: [[wiki/entities/content-rewards|Content Rewards]] (earning) and [[wiki/entities/adaptive-ai|Adaptive]] (automation / "heavy lifting").
- Core thesis: *"This isn't about working harder; it's about building a machine that studies the market while you sleep."* — automation-first, not effort-first.
- **Phase 1 — Setup (Week 1)**: Day 1, sign up for Content Rewards and launch **2-3 fresh accounts** plus a warming process. Day 2-3, market research — pick campaigns/niches you actually understand, not just "popular" ones, so you can recognize a winning hook. Day 4-7, skill acquisition — study the current meta, take an editing class focused on **high-retention cuts**.
- **Phase 2 — Automation (Week 2)** builds three named agents on Adaptive: a **Blueprint Agent** (scrapes the top 10 viral YouTube Shorts in a niche and decodes their structure into a complete blueprint), a **Strategy Extractor** (reverse-engineers the fastest-growing channels and outputs their exact playbooks), and (introduced in Phase 3) an **outlier-analysis agent** that compares viral hits against flops to explain why specific videos went viral.
- **Phase 3 — Grind (Weeks 3-4)**: post **100+ clips** across campaigns; expect ~**90% to flop** — that's the point. *"No one goes viral on their first posts."* Following the data yields a realistic **$1k-$3k in Month 1**.
- **Phase 4 — Scaling (Month 2)**: add **5 more accounts**; deploy an **Auto-Post Agent** on Adaptive that pulls edited videos from **Google Drive** and pushes them to socials automatically; hire **2-3 editors** to edit in your existing style. Editing stops being your job; scaling becomes your job.
- **Phase 5 — $10k milestone (Day 100)**: by Month 3 you're "an operator, not a creator," at **$5k-$8k/month**, with a single viral video in the right campaign able to net **$500-$1,000 overnight**. Day 100 crosses $10k.
- The differentiator framing: *"The difference between a 'struggling creator' and a 'profitable operator' is the tech stack."* — one relies on feeling the algorithm, the other uses AI agents to decode it.
- The thread is **engagement-gated**: it closes with "Follow + QT and comment 'SYSTEM' below" to receive the tool links via DM (must be following) — a lead-capture / distribution tactic, not the substance.
- The plan layers **human leverage** (hired editors) on top of **agent leverage** only after revenue exists — agents first, then headcount.
