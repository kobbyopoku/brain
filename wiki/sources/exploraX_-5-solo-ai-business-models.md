# exploraX_ (m0h) — How to Start a 6–7 Figure Solo AI Business in 2026

> m0h's full-guide survey of **five AI business models one person can run to 6-7 figures**, each anchored to a named real-world "receipt": AI influencer ([[wiki/entities/riley-brown|Riley Brown]]), AI engineer ([[wiki/entities/sajjaad-khader|Sajjaad Khader]]), AI automation ([[wiki/entities/nick-saraev|Nick Saraev]]), selling websites ([[wiki/entities/pavlo|Pavlo]]), and AI app / micro-SaaS ([[wiki/entities/marc-lou|Marc Lou]]) — closing on the claim that all five reduce to one shared bottleneck: **distribution**.

## TL;DR

m0h (the author's handle is exploraX_) argues AI is in an "overhang" — a gap between what people think AI can do and what it can actually do — and that gap is an arbitrage window that pays whoever closes it. He surveys five solo-operable business models, each with a money range and a named practitioner with public receipts, then reveals that every model's hidden dependency is distribution: building an audience that trusts you before they pay. This is the same author as [[wiki/sources/exploraX_-google-maps-leadgen]] (the Google Maps lead-gen playbook); this guide is the broader business-model survey that lead-gen post is a tactical subset of.

## Key takeaways

- **The "overhang" frame**: the gap between what people *think* AI can do (bottom line) and what it *actually* can do (top line) is large; most people are "stuck at the bottom line because they treat AI like a search bar." That gap is an arbitrage window — "when you know something the market doesn't, the market pays you to close the gap."
- **The "river of value" frame**: AI rerouted the economic river; trillions in value are moving from old workflows to AI-native ones. You don't need to build a lab — just "position yourself somewhere capital is flowing."
- **Model 1 — AI Influencer**: pick a *sub-niche* of AI ("AI" alone is too broad), pick one platform first (X for iteration, YouTube for compounding watch-time), post proven formats (workflow breakdowns, before/after demos, tool comparisons), then build-in-public → trust → brand deals → your own product. Money: X creator payouts $5-30k/mo at scale; YouTube RPM $5-15 per 1k views in AI/tech; TikTok under $1 per 1k. Receipt: [[wiki/entities/riley-brown|Riley Brown]] (0→200k followers in two weeks, $125k/mo, co-founded [[wiki/entities/vibecode|vibecode.dev]]).
- **Model 2 — AI Engineer**: highest-skill, highest-pay, most defensible. An AI engineer "takes software products and wires AI models into them" (chatbots, RAG, agents, internal tools). Roadmap framed as [[wiki/entities/sajjaad-khader|Sajjaad Khader]]'s 5-layer pyramid. Money: $200-354k+ salaried at big tech; $100-300/hr freelance; $5-15k/mo retainers. Receipt: Sajjaad Khader (Georgia Tech AI+CS master's at 20, ex-[[wiki/entities/amazon|Amazon]], 297k-subscriber YouTube channel).
- **Sajjaad Khader's 5-layer pyramid** (each layer builds on the last): (1) software foundation — Python, APIs, [[wiki/entities/git|git]], full-stack; (2) basic AI integration — call OpenAI APIs, use Hugging Face pre-trained models; (3) intelligent systems — [[wiki/entities/langgraph|LangGraph]] for multi-step workflows, [[wiki/concepts/mcp-server|MCP]] for tool access, [[wiki/concepts/retrieval-augmented-generation|RAG]] + [[wiki/concepts/vector-database|vector databases]]; (4) scale without breaking — Docker, AWS/GCP, Redis caching; (5) strategic operations — LLMOps, evaluation frameworks, cost governance, model routing.
- **Model 3 — AI Automation**: "the sweet spot" — high demand, lower skill floor than engineering, and businesses already understand why they'd pay. Wire AI into work a business already does (lead enrichment, invoice reading, ticket triage). [[wiki/entities/claude-code|Claude Code]] agentic workflows are framed as outclassing drag-and-drop no-code ([[wiki/entities/zapier|Zapier]], [[wiki/entities/make|Make]], [[wiki/entities/n8n|n8n]]) because they're natural-language, self-healing, and redeployable in seconds. Money: $500-5k setup per workflow; $1-5k/mo retainers; "five retainer clients at $3k = $15k MRR." Receipt: [[wiki/entities/nick-saraev|Nick Saraev]] ($160k/mo combined across two agencies; DO framework).
- **Model 4 — Selling Websites**: build sites for local businesses (dentists, plumbers, gyms) with Claude Code in a couple hours; charge $500-3k per build + $200-1k/mo retainer. "Building the website is the easy part. The hard part is finding a business owner who trusts you enough." The stack: [[wiki/entities/google-maps|Google Maps]] for prospects, **cold calling** to close, social media to document the journey. Receipt: [[wiki/entities/pavlo|Pavlo]] ($30k+/mo AI agency, cold-calling local businesses, endorsed by [[wiki/entities/highlevel|HighLevel]]).
- **Model 5 — AI App / Micro-SaaS**: a small niche tool ($10-49/mo) solving one painful problem, billed monthly. Claude Code + [[wiki/entities/stripe|Stripe]] + [[wiki/entities/supabase|Supabase]] + a domain ships v1 in an afternoon. Three moves: pick a niche you understand, ship in a week not a month, distribute on X. Money: $500-3k MRR starter; $5-20k MRR mid-tier; $50k+/mo portfolio. Receipt: [[wiki/entities/marc-lou|Marc Lou]] (>$1M in a single year across ShipFast/CodeFast/DataFast/TrustMRR; 35 startups shipped, ~5% hit rate).
- **The unifying thesis — distribution is the moat**: every section "quietly skipped the same question: where do the clients actually come from." The answer is the same for all five — distribution. "The people winning aren't the ones with the best skills. They're the ones who showed up consistently in front of an audience long enough that the audience trusted them with money."
- **"The overhang is closing"**: the arbitrage window narrows as the market catches up — "12 months ago you could charge $5k for an AI-built website and the client had no idea. In 12 months every business owner will know." Urgency framing: "the best time to start was last year. The second best time is today."
- **Closing-filter heuristic**: m0h pairs each model with a temperament filter (e.g. selling websites suits those who can take cold-call rejection; micro-SaaS suits those who can ship ugly and iterate) and advises picking "the path you're avoiding because it sounds hard — usually the one that matches you, because everyone else is avoiding it too."
- **Named learning resources**: Sajjaad Khader's roadmap video + paid courses; [[wiki/entities/deronin|@DeRonin_]]'s 6-month month-by-month AI-engineer breakdown; Nick Saraev's free 6-hour agentic-workflows course (DO framework: directive → orchestration → execution); Marc Lou's newsletter and "build in public + ship + tweet" playbook.

## Notes

- This is a **survey / map source**, not a how-to deep-dive. Its value to the wiki is as the connective tissue across five business-model clusters the wiki already covers in depth via single-model sources. Each of the five maps cleanly onto existing wiki concepts: AI automation → [[wiki/concepts/ai-automation-services]]; the broader frame → [[wiki/concepts/online-business-models-2026]]; the engineer roadmap touches [[wiki/concepts/retrieval-augmented-generation]] / [[wiki/concepts/mcp-server]] / [[wiki/concepts/vector-database]].
- **Same author, different scope from [[wiki/sources/exploraX_-google-maps-leadgen]].** The Google Maps lead-gen post is the *tactical* execution of this guide's "Selling Websites" and "AI Automation" client-acquisition layer. This guide is the strategic map; that one is one tactic inside it. They should cross-link.
- The **"distribution is the moat" thesis converges with multiple wiki sources**: it echoes [[wiki/concepts/horizontal-leverage]] (Saraev), the audience-first framing in the Content OS sources (shannholmberg), and the cold-calling-as-distribution claim in m0h's own lead-gen post. Worth tracking as a recurring cross-source pattern.
- **Nick Saraev's "$160k/month combined" figure** here is a tighter/cleaner number than the wiki's existing Saraev coverage — useful corroborating data point but treat as the author's claim, not independently verified. Same caution applies to all "receipt" figures (Riley Brown $125k/mo, Pavlo $30k+/mo, Marc Lou >$1M/yr) — these are second-hand claims relayed by m0h.
- The **Claude-Code-beats-no-code claim** ("agentic workflows ... self-heal when something breaks, redeployed in seconds instead of rebuilt node by node") is an opinion/positioning statement, not a benchmark. It aligns with the wiki's [[wiki/concepts/self-annealing]] concept (Saraev) — agentic/self-healing workflows as the wedge over deterministic node-graphs.
- Raw source has two visibly truncated figures (a Riley Brown raise amount and a freelance-dev cost comparison both render as empty in the clipping). Not fabricating values for these.
- **Marc Lou's "ship more, attach to none" thesis** (35 startups, 5% hit rate) is a clean articulation of the portfolio/shots-on-goal logic of micro-SaaS — candidate for a dedicated concept page if more micro-SaaS sources accumulate.
