# Matt Gittleson — I vibecoded a B2C app and exited for $375,000 in 6 months

> A non-technical solo founder's full guide to building a vibecoded B2C web app, scaling it with self-made organic UGC, and selling it for $375K inside six months — the thesis being that AI has destroyed the *build* moat, so **distribution is the only asymmetry left** and a content engine is what acquirers actually buy.

## TL;DR

Matt Gittleson built **CiteSure** (a citation/EdTech web app) nights-and-weekends around a 9-9 consulting job, could not code, and used AI builders ([[wiki/entities/cursor|Cursor]] / [[wiki/entities/claude-code|Claude Code]]) to ship the MVP. He marketed it entirely with self-produced short-form video (TikTok/Reels/Shorts) — 30M views, total spend under $100 — peaking at $132K ARR before being acquired by [[wiki/entities/jenni-ai|Jenni AI]] for $375K at ~6 months old. The load-bearing claim: *building is no longer the moat; distribution is*, and the highest-multiple exit comes from a viral content engine an acquirer cannot easily build themselves. The guide is a step-by-step on format selection, a manual content production system, churn defense (which sets the multiple), and the four acquisition channels.

## Key takeaways

- **The moat moved from product to distribution.** AI "obliterated the technical moat," so a mediocre app with outstanding distribution beats an outstanding app with mediocre distribution. Most founders still optimize the part that no longer matters.
- **Start with a format, not a problem.** Traditional ideation (find problem → validate → build → market) is "backwards and dead." Your viral potential is your ceiling — pick the viral format first, then build the minimum app the format requires.
- **A worth-picking format meets three criteria: Viral (working *right now* on TikTok, not "could work"), Scalable (50+ variations without going stale), Sustainable (already survived ≥1 fatigue cycle — hit, dipped, came back).**
- **Scroll TikTok, not Reels** — Reels run ~2 weeks behind TikTok, so by the time a format reaches Reels the early-mover edge is gone.
- **Two ways to source a format**: Path A — study apps already making real revenue and copy their winning content (e.g. Halo AI's "prank" format, UMax's face rating: *format + niche + scalable unit economics*). Path B (underused) — steal proven viral formats from ecom/dropshipping/course sellers (whose products have bad LTV/economics) and attach a better-economics, higher-retention app to the same format.
- **Pick a format that funnels to a returnable product.** A format driving a one-and-done app is a "churn machine" — views, installs, then loss in two weeks. Best formats sell habit/daily/weekly products.
- **Build with Cursor or Claude Code, not a no-granular-control builder** (which becomes painful later). Two weeks max from format to live MVP; longer means overbuilding. Build only features that appear in the format.
- **Make your first 100 videos yourself — no agents, no AI influencers, no automation, no hired creators.** Content is a learnable skill; the author had never posted a single TikTok before, and his first one got 200K views.
- **The production system**: 50 hook clips (sourced Fiverr/Upwork at <$2 each, plus friends/family for free), each demo used a max of 3 times, demos constantly re-filmed for uniqueness — total hook-clip spend under $100. 10 hooks × 10 demos ≠ 100 unique videos: TikTok's **perceptual-hashing** anti-duplication will throttle and shadowban reused demos.
- **Operational rules**: batch-film (16 hours every Sunday) so weekdays are click-to-post; run content as a portfolio (50% proven formats / 25% iterations / 25% moonshots); post **3×/day per account maximum** (more materially lowers view average, especially on TikTok).
- **"Viral sense" is the compounding meta-skill** — knowing a good video from a great one. Train it with ~30 min/day scrolling inside your niche (learn formats) + ~30 min outside ("prescribed doomscrolling," learn culture/new winners).
- **Organic crushes paid on margins.** Paid attention dies when spend stops; organic videos are perpetual assets that can go viral weeks/months later. The app ran >80% net margin with <$100 total marketing spend — near-zero CAC, which acquirers love at due diligence.
- **Retention determines your multiple.** High churn means acquirers don't treat MRR as truly recurring and pay a lower multiple. A $100K MRR app at 50% monthly churn with a broken top-of-funnel decays to ~$1.5K MRR in 6 months. Reduce churn via a genuinely good product, painkiller framing, value-education in the cancellation flow, and stay-incentives.
- **Shoot for a strategic acquisition** (higher multiples than financial, especially for young apps). Four ways to find acquirers: **Marketplaces** (Acquire/Flippa/Microns/[[wiki/entities/trustmrr|TrustMRR]] — note TTM-based pricing penalizes 6-month-old apps), **Brokers** (e.g. Empire Flippers, take a %), **Outbound** (cold outreach to an ideal-acquirer list), **Inbound** (best — no broker fees, strategic, gives you leverage; viral content inherently generates it).
- **The author's actual exit was inbound**: the [[wiki/entities/jenni-ai|Jenni]] team DM'd one of his content accounts for a *content partnership*, believing it was a real creator account — that turned into the acquisition. The buyer DM arrived when the app was <2 months old.
- **Don't skip the discomfort.** Making 500 posts and filming demos 16 hours on Sundays "sucks," but the friction is the operator skill being built — a transferable asset across every future app, hire, and role. CiteSure kept growing post-exit (~£75K/$100K ARR at sale → ~£135K/$180K ARR, ~80% growth) with no new videos posted in ~a year.

> "The hardest part of going viral is figuring out what goes viral! If someone has already done that work, copy the homework and apply it where the economics and retention are better."

## Notes

- **This is a distribution-first counterweight to most of the wiki's build-first / agent-tooling sources.** Where [[wiki/sources/saraev-agentic-workflows-2026|Saraev]] and the AI-services playbooks ([[wiki/sources/khairallah-ai-automations-10k-month]], [[wiki/sources/itsalexvacca-services-as-software-7m-agency]]) argue leverage comes from *building* automations, Gittleson argues the build is now commoditized and the entire game is *distribution + retention + exit*. Useful tension: both agree AI has collapsed the build cost; they disagree on where the residual asymmetry lives (Gittleson: organic UGC; the services crowd: orchestration/ops).
- **"Vibecoding" here is incidental, not the subject.** The headline foregrounds vibe-coding, but the substance is a content/distribution playbook. The AI-build claim ("I can't code") is a *premise* the post barely defends — it's plausible for a citation/EdTech web app but unverified as to actual codebase complexity.
- **The churn → multiple linkage is the most reusable idea for Godwin's products.** For [[wiki/projects/vedge|Vedge]] / [[wiki/projects/kivora|Kivora]] / [[wiki/projects/clarvyn|Clarvyn]], the framing "acquirers discount non-recurring-looking MRR" and "even a vitamin should feel like a painkiller" is directly applicable, though those are B2B/regulated products where the viral-UGC engine does *not* transfer.
- **Strong overlap with [[wiki/concepts/churn-at-scale|churn-at-scale]]** but inverted: that concept is agency-side (services churn defense); here it's product-side MRR churn driving acquisition multiple. Same math, different surface — worth cross-linking, not merging.
- **Unverified / single-source figures.** All numbers ($375K exit, $132K ARR peak, 30M views, <$100 spend, >80% margin, post-exit ARR growth) come solely from the author's own promotional thread. The perceptual-hashing claim about TikTok dedup is plausible and widely repeated but not independently cited here. Treat as the author's self-report.
- **The "format-first" inversion is the genuinely novel reframing** vs the wiki's existing landing-page / CRO sources, which assume the product exists and optimize conversion. Gittleson pushes the optimization one stage earlier — to *which product to even build*, chosen by its viral surface.
- **Possible concept page**: a "distribution-as-moat" / "format-first product ideation" concept doesn't exist yet and would anchor this source plus the marketing/CRO cluster. Flagged for a later phase.
