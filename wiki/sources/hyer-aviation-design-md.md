# Hyer Aviation Design System (DESIGN.md from Refero)

> *"monochromatic luxury, sharp contrast"* — private-aviation booking surface: deep almost-black elements punctuating crisp white surfaces, anchored by exceptionally large display typography (up to 187px) and a single warm accent color.

## TL;DR

Hyer Aviation's design uses a single typeface (HelveticaNowDisplay) for everything from headlines to navigation. The defining element is an extreme type scale: sizes range from 17px body up to **131px and 187px display** — among the largest in the entire 32-brand catalog. High-contrast monochromatic palette with subtle warm accent. Rounded pills temper the visual power; ample whitespace lets imagery dominate.

## Key takeaways

- **Extreme display type**: 131px and 187px sizes among the largest in the catalog — luxury-brand confidence in oversized headlines.
- **Single typeface**: HelveticaNowDisplay for all primary content (headlines, body, buttons, navigation). Single weight selection 400/700.
- **Monochromatic + one warm accent**: high-contrast white-and-near-black with restraint; warm accent serves as exception color.
- **Imagery-first composition**: ample whitespace, rounded pills, restrained palette — the design lets photography (jet imagery) take center stage.
- **Aviation context**: the aesthetic conventions match other private-aviation brands (deep contrast + giant type + photography-forward) but with the specific HelveticaNowDisplay choice giving it modern impact.

## Notable quotes

> "Monochromatic luxury, sharp contrast."

## Notes

- **One of the catalog's most extreme typographic ranges**: 17px to 187px = ~11× ratio. Most brands stay under 5×.
- **Luxury-segment design conventions**: minimalism, monochrome, oversized typography — Hyer Aviation is a textbook example of the visual conventions for high-end services.
