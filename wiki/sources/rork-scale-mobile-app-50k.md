# Rork — How to scale your mobile app to $50k+/month (The Complete Guide)

> Rork (an AI app-builder platform) publishes the distribution playbook its top builders use to take a single vibe-coded mobile app from $0 to $50k+/month — arguing that **building got easy but distribution didn't**, so the entire bottleneck is now go-to-market, not engineering.

## TL;DR

Rork's thesis: AI builders can now ship App Store apps in days, but most make no money because **distribution is the unsolved hard part**. The playbook is four sequential steps — (1) pick the right app by adding a twist to a proven app or combining two, and commit to one until it works; (2) obsess over distribution at the top of the funnel via four channels (self-made content → influencers → UGC creators → paid ads, in that order of beginner-readiness); (3) only after distribution is running, obsess over onboarding designed as a three-act story that makes users convince *themselves* they have a problem; (4) scale spend and iterate the product data-driven. The recurring discipline: spend 90-100% of time on marketing, never optimize below the top of the funnel before traffic exists, and expect to burn ~10 apps before one hits.

## Key takeaways

- **Building is solved; distribution is the moat.** "We made the build easy but distribution is still hard." Most builders will miss the wave not because they can't build but because distribution didn't get easier.
- **Crack one app first, then replicate.** Every successful builder figured out distribution, onboarding instincts, and conversion math on app #1, then went wide. Commit to one app long enough to crack distribution before shipping multiple.
- **Two ways to pick an app:** (a) take a successful app and add your own twist (personality, colors, mascot) — recommended for beginners; (b) combine two successful apps' core features (e.g. productivity + faith, fitness tracker + screen-time blocker, journaling + gamification) — harder.
- **Speed is everything:** idea-to-launch should take no more than 2 weeks using Rork.
- **The launch-day conversion signal:** a soon-to-be-successful app gets 1-5 trials or a direct conversion on launch day even on the Apple boost; if 100k social views still yield zero conversions, move to the next app idea.
- **Apps are a funnel** — distribution (views/eyeballs) at the top, paid conversion at the bottom. Never optimize anything below the top of the funnel until the distribution engine works. Animations, mascot cuteness, colors, retention, even onboarding are "a waste of time" at 100 — even 5k — downloads.
- **Four distribution channels, ordered by beginner-readiness:** make content yourself → hire influencers → hire UGC creators → paid ads. Channel choice is gated by how much money you can invest; more money = faster success.
- **Make content yourself first** even if funded — it builds your "viral sense" so you can later judge influencers and UGC creators. Post ~21 times/day = 7 posts cross-posted to TikTok, Instagram, YouTube Shorts. Copy competitor hooks, scripts, and CTAs directly.
- **Three self-made content formats:** UGC Reaction + Demo (demo ≤8 seconds; has carried apps to $10k+/mo alone), Slideshows (apps doing $20k+/mo from slideshows; needs a consistent brand identity, not random aesthetic feeds), and Memes (best on Instagram; pair with a ManyChat automation + Instagram Stories driving downloads).
- **Influencers** ($25k+/mo apps scaled this way) need a ≥$10k budget because many won't work. Mass-DM ≥100/day opening with "Paid Promo Opportunity"; vet for in-niche fit and genuine engagement (fire-emoji comments, audience that cares). Never disclose the deal in DMs — get them on a call. Target ~$1 CPM; structure as a $1 CPM trial for 300k views with 40% upfront, then move to $1.2 CPM.
- **UGC creators** are "widely considered the best way to scale mobile apps right now" — the highest-leverage and hardest channel. Not recommended for new builders without distribution experience or a co-founder; running a "UGC army" is a full-time job (recruit, monitor, track, fire fast, pay). Followers don't matter — look for creators with 2-3 viral videos, camera charisma, and communication skills. Reach out via TikTok / UGC marketplaces.
- **Paid ads** (apps gone from $2.5k/mo to $25k/mo) come last, never first. Run only after you have organic content that already worked (5 high-quality pieces/week to test) and a nailed onboarding hitting ≥10% download-to-trial. Use "Organic Ads" — genuinely organic-feeling hooks with a clear direct CTA, not comment-section conversions.
- **Onboarding is "without a doubt the most important part of your app"** — listed third only to force builders into marketing early. Design it as a three-act story: introduction painting the problem, climax where the user experiences the app, conclusion preparing for the paywall.
- **Onboarding mechanics:** at least one "aha moment" within the first minute; onboarding questions exist to make users reflect and convince *themselves* they have a problem (not to gather data); mirror answers back every few questions; longer onboarding converts better (10-15 min creates loss aversion at the paywall) as long as every screen adds value; make users actually use the core feature; show the review modal right after the core-feature moment for ASO/social proof. Iterate to ≥10% conversion.
- **Scale and iterate (Step 4):** at $50k/mo trajectory, focus on scaling distribution (more spend/UGC/influencers/content) and only now improve the product — deliberately, data-driven, optimizing back to a revenue event (e.g. trial-to-conversion) rather than adding features. Still spend 90-100% of time on marketing.
- **Failure is expected:** builders who hit $50k/mo "often went through 10 apps to find their first successful one." Obsessively focus on one app and one thing at a time.
