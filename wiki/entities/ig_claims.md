## Frameworks documented in his content

### The Conviction Gap Model

A cold prospect lands at conviction 10/100; needs 85-90/100 to sign a $5K-$15K/month retainer. Each retargeting stage adds conviction points systematically:
- **Authority proof**: +15
- **Social proof**: +20
- **Process clarity**: +10
- **Objection removal**: +15
- **Risk removal**: +20
- **Urgency**: +10

### The 5-stage retargeting funnel

| Stage | Audience | Conviction | Message |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 — Engaged cold | Visited page, no opt-in | 10-20 | Education, no selling |
| 2 — Warm engaged | Watched 50%+ of VSL or consumed lead magnet | 30-45 | Specific social proof |
| 3 — Applied/partial | Started application, didn't complete | 45-60 | Objection handling |
| 4 — Completed application, didn't book | Submitted full app, didn't schedule | 60-70 | Urgency + ease + social proof |
| 5 — Booked but no-show | Calendar booking, no-show | 50 | Re-engagement (SMS over ads) |

## Positions and claims

- **Cold traffic converts once. Retargeting converts the same people multiple times across 7-21 days until the decision to buy feels obvious.**
- **The chunk-of-prose-treated-as-unit problem also exists in retargeting** — most service businesses run *one* retargeting campaign to *everyone* who visited their site, regardless of conviction state. The fix is stage-based audience segmentation with conviction-specific creative.
- **Specific metrics from the fractional CFO client**:
  - Cold traffic: $43 cost per lead, 31% email-to-booking, **$139 per booked call**.
  - Retargeting: $8 cost per click, 68% click-to-booking, **$12 per booked call**.
  - Retargeting accounted for 23% of total booked calls at 11% of total ad spend over 36 months.
- **If your retargeting budget is < 20-25% of cold traffic budget, you're leaving closed deals on the table.**
