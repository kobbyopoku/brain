# Alex Vacca

> Co-founder of [[wiki/entities/coldiq|ColdIQ]] ($7M ARR services-as-software agency); previously at [[wiki/entities/worldcoin|Worldcoin]] for two years. Author of [[wiki/sources/itsalexvacca-services-as-software-7m-agency]] — the wiki's canonical source on [[services-as-software]] and [[churn-at-scale]].

## Profile

Alex Vacca is the founder/co-founder of ColdIQ, a bootstrapped services-as-software agency at $7M ARR / 70 clients / 30+ people across 10 countries (per his own claim, May 2026). Before ColdIQ he worked two years at Worldcoin (Sam Altman's iris-scanning company) and quit because of corporate-ceiling frustration. His operational playbook — risk reversal, first-three-hires, churn defense, documentation discipline — is the most detailed services-as-software content ingested in this wiki.

## Positions and claims

- **Services-as-software is the surviving agency shape**: agency on the contract, software underneath. *(See [[services-as-software]].)*
- **You cannot out-sell churn at scale.** *(See [[churn-at-scale]].)*
- **Only A players for the first three hires** — A delivery operator, a second delivery operator, a delivery lead.
- **Risk reversal + daily content** in combination crack the zero-proof wall; alone, neither does.
- **Pricing pressure on hour-based work compresses every operator** who hasn't built infrastructure underneath the human layer.
- **What survives LLM compression**: judgment, relationship, taste, accountability.
- **AI-layer build sequence is spine → agents → loop** — the order, not the tools/agents/stack, is what matters; he says it took him two years to understand this. *(See [[wiki/sources/itsalexvacca-3-phases-ai-layer]].)*
- **Previously argued "services don't scale"** and wrote a separate piece on "the four feedback edges." *(Per [[wiki/sources/itsalexvacca-3-phases-ai-layer]].)*
- **Demonstrated building a cold email campaign with 4 agents** in one session, end-to-end, in under an hour (video). *(Per [[wiki/sources/itsalexvacca-3-phases-ai-layer]].)*
