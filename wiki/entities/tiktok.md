# TikTok

> Short-form video platform used as a primary organic-distribution channel for AI-built apps. Appears via [[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]] and [[wiki/sources/mattgittleson-vibecoded-b2c-375k-exit]], whose production systems are shaped by TikTok's reach and anti-duplication mechanics.

## Profile

TikTok appears in this wiki as a primary distribution surface for self-made and UGC marketing content. Two sources treat it as the central channel: [[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]] uses it (with Instagram and YouTube Shorts) for high-volume cross-posting and UGC-creator outreach, while [[wiki/sources/mattgittleson-vibecoded-b2c-375k-exit]] describes how its anti-duplication detection and reach throttling shape a viral-content production system.

## Key facts

- **Self-content channel**: one of three platforms (with Instagram and YouTube Shorts) for self-made content; the Rork guide advises posting ~21 times/day across them via cross-posting ([[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]]).
- **UGC outreach**: builders reach out to UGC creators "through TikTok or UGC marketplaces" ([[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]]).
- **Virality vs. conversion**: builders can "go viral on TikTok" yet still get no conversions, signaling a wrong app ([[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]]).
- **Anti-duplication detection**: attributed in part to "perceptual hashing"; reusing a demo many times leads to throttled reach and shadowbanning ([[wiki/sources/mattgittleson-vibecoded-b2c-375k-exit]]).
- **Reels lag**: Reels run ~2 weeks behind TikTok, so the author advises scrolling TikTok rather than Reels for trend-spotting ([[wiki/sources/mattgittleson-vibecoded-b2c-375k-exit]]).
- **Posting cadence**: posting more than 3x/day per account materially decreases view average, particularly on TikTok ([[wiki/sources/mattgittleson-vibecoded-b2c-375k-exit]]).
