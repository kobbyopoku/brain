# Android

> Google's mobile operating system, reframed in its Android 17 generation as an AI-native OS in which apps become invisible.

## Profile

Android is Google's mobile operating system. In the wiki it appears through the I/O 2026 roadmap discussion, where the Android 17 generation is presented as a shift toward an AI-native operating system — one in which discrete apps recede and the OS coordinates tasks across services from a single instruction. Details below are drawn from the cited press in the source thread.

## Key facts

- **Vendor**: Google.
- Android 17 is framed as gaining Gemini intelligence, AI-native experiences, system-wide AI assistance, and conversational workflows, per the cited Android Central coverage in [[wiki/sources/shabnam-google-2026-roadmap-keynote]].

## Positions and claims

- The source depicts a single spoken instruction coordinating Maps, Gmail, Calendar, Payments, and Messages without opening individual apps — argued in [[wiki/sources/shabnam-google-2026-roadmap-keynote]].
- Read as evidence that apps become "invisible" under an AI-native OS — see [[wiki/sources/shabnam-google-2026-roadmap-keynote]].
