## Positions and claims

### From the $10K/mo playbook

- **There is a market gap**: businesses know AI exists but cannot implement; competent automation builders are scarce; this gap is worth $3-15k per project.
- **The skill-file authoring craft is the core deliverable** of an AI automation services business. *"The skill file is the engine of every automation."*
- **Pricing should be value-based, not hours-based.**
- **A ~18-month window exists** before the AI-automation services market saturates.

### From the beginner-facing "real money" course

- **AI automations for small businesses are a "gold rush"** filling the gap between businesses knowing AI exists and actually using it — argued in [[wiki/sources/eng_khairallah1-real-money-ai-automations]].
- **No software-engineering background is required** to build the AI-automation services business — see [[wiki/sources/eng_khairallah1-real-money-ai-automations]].

### From the three-session Cowork architecture

- **Conversation vs system**: *"Most people's relationship with AI is a conversation. That is not productivity. That is interruption management."*
- **Cowork is infrastructure, not a tool**: *"Build it once. Refine it weekly. Let it compound."*
- **Cowork operates on actual files**: *"It does not give you text to copy and paste. It creates the documents, updates the spreadsheets, compiles the reports, and saves everything exactly where it belongs."*
- **Specificity drives value**: *"What counts as urgent for your business is different from what counts as urgent for someone else. Define your tiers. The more specific your tiers and categories, the better the briefing."*
- **Weekly Friday refinement is the compounding loop**: 15 min/week reviewing missed items, redo work, and new automation candidates.
- **Daily savings**: 1-3 hours/day = 20-60 hours/month.
