# Brian Moran

## Profile

Brian Moran is the founder of [[wiki/entities/samcart]] (a checkout / e-commerce platform claimed at $6B+ in processed sales for 100k+ entrepreneurs and a $300M+ valuation). His public commentary draws on real top-seller data from SamCart's customer base, which gives his catalogue of "what works in 2026" some empirical grounding. In this wiki he appears as the author of the canonical source on [[online-business-models-2026]] — a 10-models-that-work + 1-that's-collapsing catalog.

## Positions and claims

- **Pure information is becoming free**, hurting digital-product sellers whose product is essentially information.
- **Human touch becomes more valuable as AI commoditizes information** — charge for experience, judgment, presence.
- **Recurring billing only survives when the product is used daily.** "If people use it once a month or less, they will cancel — it's just a matter of time."
- **Low-ticket front + high-ticket back** still works for customer acquisition.
- **Physical products are massively underrated** as an AI hedge.
- **Yearly billing for newsletters at ~$100/year** survives where $20-200/month communities die.
- **Diversify away from pure-information products before AI forces it** — 12-18 month window.
