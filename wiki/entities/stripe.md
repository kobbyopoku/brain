# Stripe

> Payments infrastructure company; YC unicorn ($65B market cap referenced); featured brand in both the [[wiki/entities/refero|Refero]] design-reference catalog and the [[wiki/sources/clear_graphics-yc-unicorn-landing-pages|YC unicorn landing-pages analysis]]. The wiki's first entity to graduate from "Refero stub" to a substantively-treated entity via secondary sources.

## Profile

Stripe is a payments infrastructure company. In this wiki it is the first entity to be cited by **two independent sources** — appearing originally as a stub via the [[wiki/entities/refero|Refero]] design catalog ("Architectural blueprint on white…"), then expanded substantively when [[wiki/sources/clear_graphics-yc-unicorn-landing-pages]] analyzed Stripe's landing page as an exemplar of multiple [[landing-page-patterns]]. *(Stripe's product surface, technical architecture, and business model still lack a primary-source ingest in this wiki — the treatments here are landing-page-design and brand-aesthetic.)*

### Landing-page patterns exemplified
Per [[wiki/sources/clear_graphics-yc-unicorn-landing-pages]]:
- **Category-defining headline**: "financial infrastructure for the internet".
- **Product-in-the-hero**: dashboard shown.
- **Social proof through scale**: "global GDP running on Stripe".
- **Minimal navigation**: 5 items.
- **Audience personalization**: developers vs. business owners.
- **Footer as navigation hub**: 50+ links organized by category.

## Positions and claims

- **A landing page should describe the category it owns, not what the product does technically** — Stripe's "financial infrastructure for the internet" is the canonical example of [[landing-page-patterns|category-defining headlines]]. *(Implicit by the source's analysis.)*
