# Optiver

> Amsterdam-based global options market maker whose open-source output centres on nanosecond-precision latency tooling.

## Profile

Optiver is an Amsterdam-based global options market maker. Its trading infrastructure is tuned for nanosecond precision, and the small slice of code it open-sources reflects that focus on latency. It appears in this wiki via a roundup of hedge-fund and quant-firm open-source repositories, where it is presented as one of the firms that publishes narrow but production-grade latency tooling rather than broad libraries.

## Key facts

- **Headquarters**: Amsterdam (cited to [[wiki/sources/zostaff-22-hedge-fund-quant-open-source-repos]]).
- **Business**: Global options market maker (cited to [[wiki/sources/zostaff-22-hedge-fund-quant-open-source-repos]]).
- **Infrastructure**: Tuned for nanosecond precision (cited to [[wiki/sources/zostaff-22-hedge-fund-quant-open-source-repos]]).
- **Open-source**: Released `timestamp9` and `optiver-asyncpg`, a production fork of `asyncpg` (cited to [[wiki/sources/zostaff-22-hedge-fund-quant-open-source-repos]]).
