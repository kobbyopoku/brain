# General Motors

> The American automaker, cited in this wiki as the 1955 exemplar of corporate dominance.

Appears in this wiki via [[wiki/sources/kushwah-aaryan-future-of-work]].

## Key facts

- In 1955, roughly one in every two hundred working Americans received a paycheck from General Motors (cited to [[wiki/sources/kushwah-aaryan-future-of-work]]).
- Its revenue was 3% of US GDP (cited to [[wiki/sources/kushwah-aaryan-future-of-work]]).
- Its CEO told the Senate that what was good for the country was good for General Motors, meaning it as a tautology not a slogan (cited to [[wiki/sources/kushwah-aaryan-future-of-work]]).
- Named alongside AT&T and IBM as a "holy trinity" of the Modern Corporation (cited to [[wiki/sources/kushwah-aaryan-future-of-work]]).
