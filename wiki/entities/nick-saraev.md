## Profile

Nick Saraev is best known in the wiki as the author of the *AGENTIC WORKFLOWS: Build & Sell AI Automations (2026)* video — a structured, exhaustive course running over 5 hours. His framing is technically architectural (the DO framework) and economically contrarian (horizontal-leverage is where AI-automation wealth concentrates, not vertical replacement). ## Positions and claims

- **Agentic workflows are "one of the largest wealth transfers in human history."** *(See [[wiki/sources/saraev-agentic-workflows-2026]].)*
- **Horizontal leverage > vertical leverage** — automating 90% of 10,000 roles creates 9,000× the value of automating 100% of one.
- **Raw LLM workflows decay multiplicatively** — chained 90%-success steps give 59% at depth 5. Solving this requires architectural separation between probabilistic reasoning and deterministic execution.
- **The DO framework gets to 2-3% error rates on real business functions** — the threshold that makes [[ai-automation-services|AI automation as a service]] economically viable.
- **Plan for graceful recovery, not perfect prevention.** Long-running agents will fail; build the loops that retry intelligently.
