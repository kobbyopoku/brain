# Instagram

> Social platform used as a distribution and outreach channel for AI-built apps, and a value-per-employee data point. Appears via [[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]] and [[wiki/sources/kushwah-aaryan-future-of-work]].

## Profile

Instagram appears in this wiki in two distinct framings. In [[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]] it is one of three self-content platforms (with TikTok and YouTube Shorts) and the preferred channel for memes, influencer outreach, and Stories-driven downloads. In [[wiki/sources/kushwah-aaryan-future-of-work]] it is a historical data point for the "value per employee" thesis: its 2012 acquisition by Facebook is used to illustrate how few employees can produce enormous deal value.

## Key facts

- **Self-content channel**: one of three platforms (with TikTok and YouTube Shorts) ([[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]]).
- **Meme + automation play**: memes "work really well on Instagram" and pair with a ManyChat automation and Instagram Stories that drive downloads ([[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]]).
- **Influencer outreach**: with influencers "you mostly want to reach out on Instagram and email" ([[wiki/sources/rork-scale-mobile-app-50k]]).
- **Acquisition**: acquired by Facebook for $1 billion in April 2012 ([[wiki/sources/kushwah-aaryan-future-of-work]]).
- **Headcount at acquisition**: thirteen employees ([[wiki/sources/kushwah-aaryan-future-of-work]]).
- **Per-employee deal value**: $77 million ([[wiki/sources/kushwah-aaryan-future-of-work]]).
