# Vannevar Bush

> American engineer who, in 1945, described the [[memex]] — a personal, associative knowledge store that prefigures the LLM Wiki pattern.

## Profile

Bush was a 20th-century American engineer and science administrator. In this wiki he appears as the conceptual ancestor of [[llm-wiki-pattern]]: his 1945 essay on the [[memex]] envisioned a private, actively curated knowledge store with associative trails between documents — closer to the LLM Wiki pattern than to what the public web became. The maintenance problem he could not solve in 1945 (who keeps the connections current?) is, in Karpathy's framing, the problem LLMs now solve.

## Key facts

- **Memex (1945)**: described a microfilm-based personal knowledge device with associative linking. See [[memex]]. Cited in [[wiki/sources/llm-wiki-pattern-karpathy]].

## Positions and claims

- **Knowledge stores should be private and actively curated**, with associative trails as first-class objects — the connections between documents being as valuable as the documents themselves. Cited via [[wiki/sources/llm-wiki-pattern-karpathy]].
