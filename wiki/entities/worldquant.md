# WorldQuant

> Quant fund behind the "101 Formulaic Alphas" paper and the crowdsourced BRAIN alpha-research platform.

## Profile

WorldQuant is a quantitative hedge fund managing roughly $9B in assets (source-reported). It turned alpha research into a crowdsourcing exercise via its BRAIN platform, and authored "101 Formulaic Alphas", described in the source as the most-cited quant work of the last decade. Despite this public footprint in research, its official GitHub presence is almost empty. It appears in this wiki via a roundup of hedge-fund and quant-firm open-source repositories.

## Key facts

- **AUM**: ~$9B, source-reported (cited to [[wiki/sources/zostaff-22-hedge-fund-quant-open-source-repos]]).
- **BRAIN platform**: Turned alpha research into crowdsourcing (cited to [[wiki/sources/zostaff-22-hedge-fund-quant-open-source-repos]]).
- **Publication**: Authored "101 Formulaic Alphas", described as the most-cited quant work of the last decade (cited to [[wiki/sources/zostaff-22-hedge-fund-quant-open-source-repos]]).
- **GitHub**: Official account is almost empty (cited to [[wiki/sources/zostaff-22-hedge-fund-quant-open-source-repos]]).
