AI developers are moving from simple 'loop engineering'—where agents plan, act, and iterate—to 'graph engineering,' using networks of specialized agents connected by data flows for complex tasks. Experts like Peter Steinberger, Carlos E. Perez, and Andrew Ng highlight how graphs enable parallelism, cross-checking, and self-improvement, predicting dominance in months. Builders see huge potential for startups, with 24/7 cloud agents, sellable SOPs, and small teams outpacing giants, though costs and reliability remain challenges.
