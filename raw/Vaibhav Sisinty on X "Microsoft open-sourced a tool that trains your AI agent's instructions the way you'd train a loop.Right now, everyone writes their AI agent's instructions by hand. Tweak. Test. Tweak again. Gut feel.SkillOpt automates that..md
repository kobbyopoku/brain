Microsoft open-sourced a tool that trains your AI agent's instructions the way you'd train a loop. Right now, everyone writes their AI agent's instructions by hand. Tweak. Test. Tweak again. Gut feel. SkillOpt automates that. It runs your agent on real tasks. Scores what worked. Analyzes what didn't. Then rewrites the instructions for you. If the new version performs better, it keeps it. If not, it rolls back and tries something else. On GPT-5.5, accuracy jumped +23.5 points. No fine-tuning. No model changes. It only changed the instructions. The output is a single markdown file. Plug it into Claude Code, Codex, Copilot, or Devin. Works across all of them. They also shipped something called SkillOpt-Sleep. It reviews your agent's past sessions overnight and improves the instructions while you're not working. Your agent literally gets better while you sleep.
