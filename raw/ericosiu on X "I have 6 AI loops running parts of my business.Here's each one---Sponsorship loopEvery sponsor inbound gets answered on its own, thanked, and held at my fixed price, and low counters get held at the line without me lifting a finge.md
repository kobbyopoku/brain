I have 6 AI loops running parts of my business. Here's each one: --- Sponsorship loop: Every sponsor inbound gets answered on its own, thanked, and held at my fixed price, and low counters get held at the line without me lifting a finger. The wild part: after each batch it rewrites its own instructions, and on one run it added rate-card handling, usage rights, and video-bundle scoping I never asked for. --- Conversion loop: It ships one conversion test a week on my site, measures what moved, logs it, and reports back every Monday, all against a target of a 30% lift. Because it remembers each test, four weeks compound into a measurable gain instead of the same experiment run over and over like a /goal command would. --- Content loop: It ingests my long-form videos and Gong call recordings, pulls the sharpest moments, and drafts posts around ideas that are early and low on search volume before they trend. The X writer inside it is the one that took an account from 87k to 3M+ impressions a month. --- LinkedIn loop: It runs a stolen 5M-impressions-a-month play on repeat: give away a resource, ask for a one-word comment, then DM the ICP-fit people who reply. It keeps a few post variants and tunes which angle pulls the most comments over time. --- Sweeps loop: A set of loops that verify instead of build: they scan my repos for security holes, check my site daily for broken forms and dead links, and keep my CRM clean. They catch the revenue leaks that never show up until you go looking for them. --- Revenue command center: Every morning it reads my CRM, calls, and pipeline and hands me the stale deals, the lost deals, and the exact follow-up to send each one. I skim the list and approve, it sends, and it learns which messages get replies over time.
