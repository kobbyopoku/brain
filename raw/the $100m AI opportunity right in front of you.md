a couple of months ago, i sat in on a meeting at this huge company, one with an $8 billion market valuation, the kind of company you'd assume has everything figured out by now.

the CEO was at the head of the table with around 50 of his employees around it, and one of his guys at the front was running a session for the team on AI. and for the entire hour, he was teaching them the history of AI and how to use chatgpt and how to write a prompt. basic stuff most of us learned a year and a half ago, and they were sitting through it like it was brand new the CEO looked unimpressed the whole time. people were on their phones, not paying attention, laughing about something else. a couple guys pitched before me and nobody in the room really paid attention to them either

then it was my turn

i told them, pretty bluntly, how far behind they actually are and how fast things are moving right now. i told them what a single AI agent could do today in the way they're currently running things. and the whole room went quiet. like properly quiet. the CEO's face changed and i could see something click for him in real time. and the employees got quiet for a completely different reason, they suddenly realised one agent could probably do what they were sitting in that room learning about

that meeting genuinely flipped a switch in my head

because if an $8 billion company is this far behind, what does the rest of the economy actually look like

there are millions of businesses out there running on excel files and manual workflows, so inefficient. the people who actually understand AI right now, who actually know what's possible in 2026, are this tiny little bubble. twitter feels massive because it feels like we're all together in the same room, but the second you step outside it almost no business owner has ever built an agent or used claude or has any clue what AI can do for them

that gap is the whole opportunity, and the opportunity isn't raising $50M for some AI wrapper. it's just walking into a boring business and automating one painful thing for them

let me give you three examples from my own work so you know i'm not just talking

first one was a concrete contractor. one guy running everything himself, picking up over 100 calls a day, pricing jobs over the phone, having the same conversation a hundred times.

so we built him a voice agent. now when someone calls, the agent picks up and actually has a real conversation with them, figures out what kind of job they want, works out a quote, and then texts him a message that says "quote is [$X](https://x.com/search?q=%24X&src=cashtag_click). press 1 to send, 2 to modify, 3 to call them back." he still owns every decision. he just doesn't have to be the phone anymore

second one was a company that makes boats. their invoicing was a complete mess. everything typed into excel by hand and orders getting lost in spreadsheets. the owner was spending hours every single week just trying to keep the workflow from falling apart

so we built them a dashboard that runs the whole thing automatically. clean structure, every order in one place, every number where it should be

third one is honestly pretty cool. this client sells luxury golf carts and wanted to start going after churches as a customer because churches buy in fleets and the tickets are big

so we built him a system that pulls google satellite and street view of every church, renders a mockup of his cart sitting on their actual property, puts the church's own logo onto the cart, and then mails it out to them as a physical postcard

so a lead opens his mail and sees a personalised photo of his own church with a custom branded golf cart sitting on the lawn. that's not cold outreach anymore. that's something he's going to remember

so that's three businesses, all boring on paper, all painful for the owner until someone walked in and said i can automate this one thing for you

now think about how many businesses are out there like this. literally millions of them. concrete, dental, roofing, hvac, landscaping, plumbing, accounting, manufacturing, real estate, law firms, anything where the owner is doing the same task 50 times a day and hating every minute of it

find a boring business. spend 30 minutes with the owner. ask them what they hate doing every single day. could be calls, quotes, invoices, follow-ups, lead gen, scheduling, whatever it is. pick one of those, build the agent for it, and charge them for it. that's the whole game

and if you're sitting there thinking "i don't even know how to build this stuff", you're way closer than you think. AI lets you build things over a weekend that would've taken a team of engineers two years to ship. the bottleneck isn't technical anymore, it's just seeing the opportunity and actually going out and talking to a real business

the massive companies will figure it out eventually. they'll hire consultants and sit through more hour-long lectures and slowly catch up over the next two years. but the millions of small businesses underneath them are all just sitting there waiting. they don't even know they're waiting yet

that's the $100m opportunity. it's in every town in the world and right now almost nobody is actually doing it
