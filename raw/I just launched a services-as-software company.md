Yesterday I launched [@Frontal\_AI](https://x.com/@Frontal_AI), a services-as-software company.

Frontal builds the revenue engine behind a company's sales and marketing. The clean data, the buying signals, and the plays that turn a signal into a booked meeting. We build it inside your own tools, so you own it, and we either hand it over or keep running it for you. Most of it runs on AI. The whole point is to grow revenue without hiring more people to chase it.

None of this is new work for us. Frontal is the agency side of ColdIQ, which we've run for three years. We took it out, gave it its own name, and opened it up to the whole market instead of just our own clients.

## How we got here

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HMPFFbUbsAIl-5e?format=jpg&name=large)

I joined Michel at ColdIQ three years ago, back when it was a Slack channel and a handful of clients. Someone would hand us their pipeline problem, we'd run their outbound, and they'd pay us for the meetings we booked. Cold email, LinkedIn, cold calling, all by hand. One client, then ten, then a few hundred. We got to 275+ companies and $7M ARR that way.

At some point the agency stopped being a group of people sending emails and turned into a machine. We never planned to build software. We just ran the same steps so many thousands of times that it got obvious how to hand each one off. A step we'd done by hand enough became a checklist, then a prompt, then an agent. Today around 80% of the outbound runs itself.

That machine is [@Frontal\_AI](https://x.com/@Frontal_AI).

## What we actually do

The core of it is an engine that runs on signals. Something happens that means a company is in the market: they raise a round, someone from an account keeps landing on your pricing page, a champion of yours changes jobs. The engine catches that signal, enriches and scores it, and runs the right play, the email, the LinkedIn touch, the call, without anyone doing it by hand.

We build that engine inside your own tools, on your own data, and you own it. There's no black box and no big upfront bill for a trickle of leads you can't explain. If we ever stop working together, the working system stays with you.

On top of that engine we run three things, and you can take one or all three.

- GTM engineering is the outbound that generates pipeline now.
- Ads engineering warms up cold accounts before anyone calls them.
- Content engineering builds the authority that makes inbound come to you.

> **Run one and it works. Run all three and they feed each other, so every cycle gets sharper and every meeting costs less.**

We don't promise a lead count. Anyone who does just ends up sending more email, which is the exact volume game you're trying to get out of. What we promise is your first play live in your own tools within 30 days, or your first month is free.

And you don't hire anyone to make it happen.

Normally more pipeline means more SDRs, and more clients mean more account managers. We spent three years breaking that math on ourselves. 65% of our own inbound comes from LinkedIn. We book 120 meetings a month off people who did nothing but like or comment on a post. Last month that was 500+ leads and 110+ calls, without a single DM. We run the exact thing we sell.

## The part we don't automate

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HMPI0e-bsAIROem?format=jpg&name=large)

And we're hiring, btw. You can join us.

We leave about 20% of the work with a person, on purpose. That's the part that needs judgment.

A person needs to know not to email an account the week its VP got walked out. When three prospects in a row go quiet on the same line, someone has to catch it and rewrite the offer. Sometimes it's just reading a message and knowing it's wrong before any model would. Take that person out and the whole thing gets faster and worse at once. Keeping them in is why we still call it a service.

## Where this is going

Sequoia and Y Combinator have both written about this lately.

About how companies spend about six dollars on services for every dollar they spend on software. About how AI can now do that services work, so the winners will sell the finished outcome instead of the tool.

YC on the other hands thinks the biggest vertical AI companies will become the AI version of an industry rather than sell software into it.

They're right. The only difference is timing. We were already running it before either of them wrote it down.

And there's a real reason it works, one you can watch in the numbers. Work that used to need a full team now runs mostly on agents with a couple of operators on top, and it books more than it did by hand. Every time the models get better, it costs us less to deliver and we hold the price where it is.

So that's Frontal. If you still add a head every time you want more revenue, come talk to us.
