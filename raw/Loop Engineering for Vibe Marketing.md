Everyone's building products with loops. Ellie runs his whole business on them.

Loop engineering blew up on Twitter about a month ago. Boris from Claude Code and Peter Steinberger started posting about it, and the term stuck.

Most people point loops at code. Build, verify, repeat until the feature works.

Ellie came on The Startup Ideas Podcast to show a bigger idea: point the same loop at your whole business.

SEO. Ads. Product feedback. Support. Each one runs on a loop while you do other things.

## The loop is old. The operator is new.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HNIKrHubYAACgwJ?format=jpg&name=large)

Fifteen years ago, The Lean Startup gave us build, measure, learn. Eric Ries pulled it from Toyota's assembly line, where a tight cycle produced reliable cars at a good price.

Swap the human operator for an agent and you get three steps:

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HNIK02KbcAAH5ap?format=jpg&name=large)

- **Build:** tell the AI what to make or change.
- **Verify:** check it against an objective metric.
- **Loop:** feed the result back and run it again.

The verify step trips people up. It can be any number the world hands back to you, from a test score to a search rank.

> A loop without a stop condition just burns tokens. A loop with an objective metric runs your business.

## Start with SEO, because the metric is black and white

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HNIKxo8bIAANNST?format=jpg&name=large)

Your Google rank moved up this month, or it moved down, or it stayed. No debate.

Inbox Zero, the AI inbox product Ellie runs, ranks first for "inbox zero." For "AI email assistant" it sits around position 30. That gap is the loop's whole job.

Here's the setup:

1. **Give the agent your data.** Connect Google Search Console (it has an API and shows exactly where you rank) and Data for SEO (competitor rankings, similar to Ahrefs or Semrush).
2. **Run a free audit first.** Meta tags, JSON-LD, a missing sitemap. Quick wins most sites leave on the table.
3. **Make it remember.** Keep a markdown file of every change. Next run, it checks its own experiment: "I rewrote that description. Did the page climb or drop?"
4. **Automate the schedule.** Claude routines, Cursor automations, Codex automations. Every few weeks it picks up where it left off.

The honest catch: SEO pays out in months, not days. Ellie ran this on [DraftFantasy.com](https://draftfantasy.com/), a site he started twelve years ago that lit up during the World Cup with ten million impressions in three months. One term pulls 120,000 clicks at position four. Push that to two, and it could be half a million.

> Wake up on month four and suddenly you're on page one. That's how compounding feels.

## The cost question, answered

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HNIK9acaUAAeuu7?format=jpg&name=large)

A front-end engineer named Ross Mike went on the pod recently and called the hype overblown. His take: the token providers get rich while everyone else burns money.

Fair for heavy engineering loops. (Peter Steinberger now works at OpenAI and reportedly spends $1.3M a month on credits.)

The SEO loop is the opposite. It runs once a month and can cost under $5 a run. Compare that to an agency retainer.

On a $100 or $200 max plan, you have tens of thousands of dollars of usage sitting there. On a $20 plan, watch your tokens and reach for a cheaper open model like GLM 5.2.

Want a guardrail? Price your outcomes. A click is worth three cents. A customer is worth $100. Tell the loop to stop when the math turns against you.

## The same shape works everywhere

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HNILEV3a4AAUrTP?format=jpg&name=large)

**Ads loop.** The AI writes three variants, reads the data, cuts the losers, doubles down on the winner. Same play as an ads agency. Copy changes are easy for AI. Video and graphics still lean on a human. The move: record a 30-second clip yourself, let the AI cut angles from it. Human feeling, AI optimization.

**Product feedback loop.** Ellie calls this the ultimate one. An agent reads customer feedback, watches PostHog analytics and Sentry logs, finds the biggest pain point, ships a fix, then checks retention or DAU. Run bugs and features as two separate loops: uptime for bugs, core metrics for features.

**Social loop.** Skip the fantasy of an agent farming 100,000 followers. Start with the minimal viable loop. Ellie posts ten tweets a week. Nine flop, one lands. The whole game is asking why that one worked, then doing it again. Your metric is ten likes, not a follower count.

## The takeaway

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HNILLy6a0AArh-N?format=jpg&name=large)

You already run these loops in your head. You wake up, look at yesterday's numbers, and try to do better today. You are the agent starting the loop.

The shift is handing the boring cycle to something that runs at 3am and remembers everything.

> Anything you can do at a computer, an AI can loop. The only question is which loop you hand it first.
