Most "make money online" advice is outdated.

I've been watching online businesses succeed and fail for 15 years. I built my first business from a basement apartment while working a government job I hated. That business turned into multiple seven figures. Then I built SamCart, which has now processed over $6 billion in sales for 100,000+ entrepreneurs.

I'm not telling you this to brag. I'm telling you because **I see what's actually working in real time.**

Real data from real businesses making real money.

And here's what I can tell you: **the world is about to get turned upside down.**

AI is changing everything. The amount of information anyone can get with a $30 ChatGPT or Claude account is insane. Pure information is becoming free, and that's going to hurt a lot of digital product sellers who are basically just selling information.

But here's the thing, opportunity is everywhere if you know where to look.

Let me show you the 10 models that will actually work in 2026, and more importantly, the ones that are about to die.

## The Business Model That's About to Collapse

Before I tell you what works, I need to warn you about what's dying.

**Monthly communities.**

I'm watching it happen in real time. The $20 to $200 per month memberships with thousands of members and almost no engagement? They're dead, and they just don't know it yet.

Let me explain.

You launch a community.

You're good at marketing, so you get a bunch of people in.

Month one looks great. Month two, a few people cancel. Month three, more cancel. By month six, you're on a treadmill trying to acquire new members just to replace the ones leaving.

That’s called CHURN, and it’s killed more businesses than I can count.

**Why? Because most communities are not alive. They are dead.**

There's a little bit of engagement. They've got 5,000 members and 20 people talking. The other 4,980 are paying money for something they don't even use once a month.

Why would they keep paying?

They won't.

Churn will kill these businesses this year, I'd bet on it. The only time recurring works is if you have a tool or piece of software people use every single day. Most communities don't even get used once a month.

**If I had to pick the last business model I would start right now, it would be a monthly community.**

Now let me show you what actually works.

## 1\. High-Ticket Coaching ($2K - $10K+)

This is where the real money is, and it's only getting more valuable.

I was just looking at our top sellers.

The ones printing money aren't selling a $39 course. They're selling $2,000, $4,000, $5,000 offers. Access to themselves, with real coaching, and a real transformation.

This works because the **human touch is becoming MORE valuable as AI takes over information.**

Think about it. If anyone can get information for free from ChatGPT, what becomes scarce? What becomes valuable?

**You**.

Your experience, your perspective, and your ability to look at someone's specific situation and tell them exactly what to do.

AI can give generic advice. It can't replace a coach who's been where you're trying to go, looking at your business, and saying "here's what I would do if I were you."

That's worth $5,000, $10,000, and more.

The people I know who are killing it right now all have some version of this. A high-ticket backend where their most committed customers can get real access.

If you have expertise and you're not offering something at the [$2K](https://x.com/search?q=%242K&src=cashtag_click)\+ level, you're leaving serious money on the table.

## 2\. Low-Ticket Digital Products ($7 - $97)

Wait, didn't I just say information is becoming free?

Yes. But low-ticket products aren't about the information. **They're about building a customer list.**

Ebooks. Mini-courses. Templates. Checklists. Little apps.

These work because they're impulse buys. Low commitment and an easy yes. Someone sees your offer, thinks "sure, that's worth $27," and buys without thinking too hard.

Now they're a customer. Not a lead. A customer.

And customers are worth 10x more than leads because they've already pulled out their credit card. They've already said yes to you once, so the second yes is so much easier.

**This is the entry point to your business, not the business itself.**

Use low-ticket to acquire customers cheaply. Then sell them the high-ticket backend. That's the model that's been working for a decade and will keep working.

The mistake people make is thinking the low-ticket product IS the business. It's not. It's customer acquisition.

## 3\. Newsletters with Yearly Billing ($100/year)

Agora figured this out years ago and built a billion-dollar publishing empire.

Their "community" is basically a newsletter. You're paying for access to their research, their stock picks, their insights, and it's about $100 a year.

**That's an impulse buy.**

The key is **yearly billing**.

When you bill monthly, you're forcing a decision every 30 days. "Is this worth it? Should I cancel?" Every month, that question comes up.

When you bill yearly, that question comes up once, and if they're getting enough value, or honestly if they just forgot about it, they'll keep it.

I'm not saying to trick people. I'm saying to price and bill in a way that removes friction.

$100/year for something valuable? Easy yes. Keep it forever.

$10/month for the same thing? Constant decision points. Constant churn.

**If you're going to do recurring, go low-ticket and annual. Not monthly.**

## 4\. Supplements and Physical Products

If you look at our top sellers right now on SamCart, supplements are at the top.

I know what you're thinking. "Brian, I'm a course creator. I don't know anything about physical products."

I get it. Physical products are overlooked because they're hard to get into if you've never done it. There's inventory, fulfillment, manufacturing. It's a different world.

But here's what keeps me up at night:

**You could wake up 12 to 18 months from now, and your course sales are gone.**

AI is getting better every day. The information in your course might be freely available to anyone with an internet connection. What then?

If you don't have something non-digital in the works, there's a real chance you're going to regret it.

Physical products can't be replicated by AI. Someone still has to make the thing and ship the thing.

I'm not saying abandon digital. I'm saying **diversify before you're forced to.**

If I were a digital product seller right now and I was bored or looking for the next thing, physical products would be at the top of my list.

## 5\. Build Your Own Apps

This one is brand new, and most people are sleeping on it.

With AI's ability to help you code now, **you don't need to be a developer to build software.**

Claude Code, Cursor, and Replit. These tools let almost anyone build functional apps.

I built an iOS app in an hour when my engineering team said it would take months.

Think about what this means for digital product sellers.

Instead of selling a PDF, you could sell an app that delivers your content in a more engaging way.

Instead of a static course, you could build a tool that helps people implement what you're teaching.

Instead of competing with free AI information, you could build something AI can't replicate.

**The digital product sellers who figure this out will have a massive advantage over everyone else.**

This is the opportunity hiding in plain sight.

## 6\. In-Person Events

These are high-touch, high-ticket, and impossible to replicate with AI.

If you have an audience, run an event.

Charge premium prices, and create an experience people can't get anywhere else.

There's something about getting people in a room together that no Zoom call or online community will ever replace. The connections, the energy, and the focus are all on a different level.

**And these events compound your brand.**

You charge $2,000 to $5,000 for a ticket. You make money on the event itself. But more importantly, you create superfans because you deepen relationships, create content, and generate testimonials.

Events are backend money that pays dividends for years.

If you're not doing at least one event a year, you're missing out.

## 7\. Exclusive High-Ticket Communities ($5K+/year)

I said communities are dying. Let me clarify.

Cheap communities are dying, but expensive ones are thriving.

The only community model that works is super high-ticket. We're talking $5,000+ per year. Real access to you as a thought leader.

Small group. High engagement. Exclusivity.

This works because half the reason people buy is identity.

When someone pays $5,000 to be in your inner circle, they're buying status, belonging, and the identity of "I'm the type of person who's in this room."

So you need to make it expensive, exclusive, and worth it.

Don't try to get 5,000 members at $50/month. Work hard to get 50 members at $5,000/year.

It’s less of a headache, more profit, and happier customers.

## 8\. Software and Tools (Daily Use)

The only recurring model I would start from scratch.

Ask yourself: **do people use it every day?**

If yes, recurring works. If not, don't even try.

Think about the subscriptions you've kept for years. Netflix, Spotify, your email tool, your project management software.

These are things you use constantly.

Now think about the ones you've canceled. That meditation app you used twice, that fitness community you forgot about, or that tool you signed up for and never logged into again.

**Usage is everything.**

If you can build something people genuinely can't live without, something they open every single day, you've got a real recurring business.

If people use it once a month or less, they will cancel. It's just a matter of time.

## 9\. Investing and Financial Advice

One of our top sellers right now is a newsletter and portfolio access offer.

"The last gold portfolio you'll ever need."

High-ticket. [$2K](https://x.com/search?q=%242K&src=cashtag_click), [$4K](https://x.com/search?q=%244K&src=cashtag_click), [$5K](https://x.com/search?q=%245K&src=cashtag_click) to get access to their investment research and recommendations.

**People will always pay for help with their money.** Especially when everything feels uncertain.

There's something about financial anxiety that makes people willing to spend. If you can help them feel more secure, more informed, and more in control of their financial future, they'll pay premium prices.

This niche isn't going anywhere. If anything, it's getting bigger.

## 10\. AI Services for Businesses

This is the opportunity that's printing money right now.

Most businesses are completely behind on AI. They know it's important, they've read the headlines, but they have no idea how to actually implement it.

**If you can build AI workflows, automations, and little apps for businesses that are lagging behind, you can charge premium prices.**

This is a service, not a product, but the demand is insane.

Walk into any traditional business and ask "what tasks do your employees do over and over that a computer could do?" They'll give you a list of 50 things.

Then build the automation. Charge [$5K](https://x.com/search?q=%245K&src=cashtag_click), [$10K](https://x.com/search?q=%2410K&src=cashtag_click), [$20K](https://x.com/search?q=%2420K&src=cashtag_click) and then move on to the next business.

This won't last forever because eventually everyone will catch up. But right now? Still wide open.

## What I'd Avoid in 2026

Let me be direct about what I wouldn't touch:

**Monthly communities at $10-$200/month.** Churn will kill you because the business model is broken.

**Info products that AI can replace.** If someone can get the same information from ChatGPT, your product is in trouble.

**Anything where people don't use it daily but pay monthly.** The math doesn't work long-term.

**Get-rich-quick schemes.** They never worked, and they still don't work.

If you look at everything I just shared, there's a clear pattern.

**The human touch is becoming MORE valuable, not less**. As AI takes over information, what you can charge for is your experience, your judgment, and your presence.

**Low-ticket front end into high-ticket backend still works.** Acquire customers cheaply, then offer them the premium transformation.

**Recurring only works if people use it daily.** Stop trying to force monthly subscriptions on things people use once a month.

**Physical products are massively underrated.** Everyone's chasing digital because it's easy but that's exactly why physical has less competition.
