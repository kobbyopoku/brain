1. **pick a niche so small it feels stupid** the smaller it feels, the less competition you're fighting and the easier it is to become the obvious choice.
2. **talk to 20 users before you write a single line of code** you'll waste way less time building things nobody actually wants.
3. **ship the ugly version in week 1, not month 3** speed to feedback beats polish every single time early on.
4. **charge from day 1, free trials just delay the truth** if nobody pays now, a "free" period won't change that later.
5. **your first 10 customers come from DMs, not ads** ads need volume to work, DMs just need you to show up.
6. **build in public, the accountability alone doubles your output** posting your numbers publicly makes it way harder to quietly quit.
7. **make onboarding so simple your mom could do it** every extra step is a chance for someone to bounce before they see the value.
8. **add viral loops to your product** a simple "share your result" button or a "powered by" link baked into the product beats another feature nobody asked for.
9. **respond to support tickets in under 1 hour for the first 100 users** that speed is what turns a random signup into a fan who tells their friends.
10. **your pricing page is a product, treat it like one** it's the single highest-leverage page on your whole site.
11. **annual plans fix cashflow faster than any growth hack** one upfront payment can fund a month of building instead of chasing MRR month to month.
12. **churn kills you slower than no growth, but it kills you** you can survive a slow start, you can't survive a leaky bucket forever.
13. **every unsubscribe gets a personal email asking why** it's the cheapest research you'll ever do, and sometimes it wins them back.
14. **your best feature requests come from users** your own roadmap literally is just a guess, the user is a fact. (I use [Upvoty](https://upvoty.com/) for this)
15. **SEO compounds, ads don't, pick one to obsess over** ads stop the second you stop paying, content keeps working while you sleep. ads are perfect for quick wins and quick testing tho
16. **a waitlist before launch beats a launch with no waitlist** you want day 1 to feel loud, not empty.
17. **Twitter/X threads outperform blog posts for early traction** the algorithm rewards you instantly, Google makes you wait months.
18. **build an integration for at least 1 big tool in your niche** 1 good integration can bring more users than 10 new native features because you'll be instantly discovered by new users from their marketplaces
19. **reviews on your pricing page convert better than testimonials on your homepage** people read reviews right when they're deciding to pay, not before.
20. **the demo video should be 60 seconds, not 6 minutes** nobody's giving you 6 minutes of attention before they've paid you a dollar.
21. **affiliate 30% recurring gets you free marketers** don't you dare to pay less, let them GRIND!
22. **a Slack/Discord community turns users into evangelists** people who feel like insiders start promoting your product for free.
23. **ship a changelog publicly, it builds trust weekly** it shows you're still in the game, even when nothing feels like it's happening.
24. **your product hunt launch is 1 day, your content is forever** don't put all your growth eggs into a 24 hour basket.
25. **track activation rate, not signups, signups lie** a signup that never comes back was never really a user.
26. **raise prices before you think you're ready** if nobody's pushed back on price yet, you're probably charging too little.
27. **the $10k mark isn't a feature problem, it's a distribution problem** most founders keep building when they should be posting. Divide your time like this: 80% marketing / 10% building. Use the other 10% for eating, sleeping, and family.
